# Literature Review: Foresight and Design

## Overview

Foresight is a professional discipline focused on anticipating possible futures to inform present-day actions and strategic decisions. When meeting design research, foresight contributes systematic methods — horizon scanning, trend analysis, scenario planning, backcasting — that complement design's material and speculative capacities. The intersection produces design foresight: a practice that materializes futures through design while grounding speculation in systematic analysis.

**Observatory Connection:** The Observatory links Foresight to Speculative Design and Design Fiction, noting it employs techniques like Horizon Scanning, Trend Analysis, and Backcasting that complement design research approaches.

## Foundational Futures Studies

1. **Dator, J. (2009). Alternative Futures at the Manoa School.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 14(2), 1–18.
   URL: https://jfsdigital.org/articles-and-essays/2009-2/vol-14-no-2-november/articles/alternative-futures-at-the-manoa-school/
   - Overview of the Manoa School; four generic futures images (continuation, collapse, discipline, transformation) used widely in design futures.

2. **Bell, W. (1997). Foundations of Futures Studies: Human Science for a New Era, Volume 1.** Transaction Publishers.
   ISBN: 978-1-56000-271-4
   - Comprehensive philosophical and methodological foundations for futures studies.

3. **Slaughter, R. A. (1995). The Foresight Principle: Cultural Recovery in the 21st Century.** Praeger.
   ISBN: 978-0-275-95259-0
   - Argued foresight is a necessary cultural capacity; foundational for connecting foresight to design.

4. **Voros, J. (2003). A Generic Foresight Process Framework.** *Foresight*, 5(3), 10–21.
   DOI: 10.1108/14636680310698379
   - Widely used foresight process framework; the "futures cone" extended Dator's work and influenced design's PPPP cone.

5. **Inayatullah, S. (1998). Causal Layered Analysis: Poststructuralism as Method.** *Futures*, 30(8), 815–829.
   DOI: 10.1016/S0016-3287(98)00086-X
   - CLA: a layered futures methodology moving from litany to metaphor/myth. Adapted by design researchers for deeper speculation.

## Foresight Meets Design

6. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
   URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
   - Bridged foresight and design through experiential futures: making futures tangible through designed experiences.

7. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
   DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
   - Detailed case study where foresight methods (scenario development) meet design practice (experiential staging).

8. **Candy, S., & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3–22.
   DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
   - Combining foresight with ethnographic methods; futures practice grounded in lived experience.

9. **Cuhls, K. E. (2020). Horizon Scanning in Foresight — Why Horizon Scanning Is Only a Part of the Game.** *Futures & Foresight Science*, 2(1), e23.
   DOI: 10.1002/ffo2.23
   - Clarified horizon scanning within foresight; relevant to how design researchers use foresight methods.

## Design Foresight as Integrated Practice

10. **Fry, T. (2009). Design Futuring: Sustainability, Ethics and New Practice.** Berg Publishers.
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474293488
    - "Design futuring" and "defuturing": design is central to whether futures are sustained or destroyed. Bridges foresight's analytical stance with design's material agency.

11. **Willis, A.-M. (2006). Ontological Designing.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 4(2), 69–92.
    DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514
    - Design designs us back (ontological designing); the futural dimension of all design. Theoretical bridge between foresight and design practice.

12. **Irwin, T. (2015). Transition Design: A Proposal for a New Area of Design Practice, Study, and Research.** *Design and Culture*, 7(2), 229–246.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829
    - Transition design uses foresight methods (backcasting, visioning) as core components of design for societal transitions.

13. **Mazé, R. (2019). Politics of Designing Visions of the Future.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 23–38.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0003
    - Analyzed the political dimensions of design-led futuring; who gets to envision whose future.

## Scenario Methods in Design

14. **Schwartz, P. (1991). The Art of the Long View: Planning for the Future in an Uncertain World.** Doubleday.
    ISBN: 978-0-385-26732-5
    - Classic on scenario planning; widely adopted in design-led futuring.

15. **Ilstedt, S., & Wangel, J. (2014). Altering Expectations: How Design Fictions and Backcasting Can Leverage Sustainable Lifestyles.** *Proceedings of DRS '14*.
    DOI: 10.21606/drs.2014.17
    - Explicit integration of backcasting (foresight method) with design fiction.

16. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
    - The PPPP futures cone adapted from Voros (2003); foresight's conceptual framework entering design through Speculative Everything.

17. **Gaziulusoy, A. İ., & Erdoğan Öztekin, E. (2019). Design for Sustainability Transitions: Origins, Attitudes and Future Directions.** *Sustainability*, 11(13), 3601.
    DOI: 10.3390/su11133601
    - Systematic mapping of design for sustainability transitions; foresight methods as tools for transition design.

## Critical Perspectives on Futures

18. **Appadurai, A. (2013). The Future as Cultural Fact: Essays on the Global Condition.** Verso Books.
    ISBN: 978-1-84467-985-5
    - The capacity to aspire is unequally distributed; relevant to justice in design foresight.

19. **Schultz, T., Abdulla, D., Ansari, A., Canlı, E., Keshavarz, M., Kiem, M., Prado de O. Martins, L., & Vieira de Oliveira, P. J. S. (2018). What Is at Stake with Decolonizing Design? A Roundtable.** *Design and Culture*, 10(1), 81–101.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2018.1434368
    - Whose futures? Decolonizing critique of Western-centric futuring in design.

20. **Escobar, A. (2018). Designs for the Pluriverse: Radical Interdependence, Autonomy, and the Making of Worlds.** Duke University Press.
    DOI: 10.1215/9780822371816
    - Decolonial design futures; multiple worldings rather than a single foresight frame.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **Speculative Design**: Foresight provides the analytical framework (scenarios, cones) that Speculative Design materializes
- **Design Fiction**: Foresight scenarios become Design Fiction worlds
- **Transition Design**: Uses foresight methods (backcasting, visioning) as core practice
- **Futuring Methods**: Foresight is the professional discipline feeding design's engagement with futures
- **World Building**: Foresight scenarios provide the factual grounding for fictional world construction

---

## Summary

Foresight brings systematic futures methods (horizon scanning, scenario planning, backcasting, CLA) to design research. The intersection — through Candy's experiential futures, Fry's design futuring, and Irwin's transition design — creates practices that are both analytically rigorous and materially grounded. The Dunne & Raby PPPP cone, itself adapted from Voros's foresight framework, exemplifies how foresight concepts have been absorbed into design vocabulary.
