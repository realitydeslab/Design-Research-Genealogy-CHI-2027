## Foundational Works

1. **Dator, J. (2009). Alternative Futures at the Manoa School.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 14(2), 1–18.
   URL: https://jfsdigital.org/articles-and-essays/2009-2/vol-14-no-2-november/articles/alternative-futures-at-the-manoa-school/
   — Overview of the Manoa School; four generic futures images (continuation, collapse, discipline, transformation) used widely in design futures.

2. **Inayatullah, S. (1998). Causal Layered Analysis: Poststructuralism as Method.** *Futures*, 30(8), 815–829.
   DOI: 10.1016/S0016-3287(98)00086-X
   — CLA: a layered futures methodology moving from litany → systemic causes → worldview → myth/metaphor. Adapted by design researchers for deeper speculation.

3. **Voros, J. (2003). A Generic Foresight Process Framework.** *Foresight*, 5(3), 10–21.
   DOI: 10.1108/14636680310698379
   — Widely used foresight process framework; the "futures cone" extended Dator's work and influenced design's PPPP cone (via Dunne & Raby).

4. **Bell, W. (1997). Foundations of Futures Studies: Human Science for a New Era, Volume 1.** Transaction Publishers.
   ISBN: 978-1-56000-271-4
   — Comprehensive philosophical and methodological foundations for futures studies.

5. **Slaughter, R. A. (1995). The Foresight Principle: Cultural Recovery in the 21st Century.** Praeger.
   ISBN: 978-0-275-95259-0
   — Argued foresight is a necessary cultural capacity; foundational for connecting foresight to design.

6. **Schwartz, P. (1991). The Art of the Long View: Planning for the Future in an Uncertain World.** Doubleday.
   ISBN: 978-0-385-26732-5
   — Classic on scenario planning; widely adopted in design-led futuring as a method for constructing alternative futures.

## Foresight Meets Design

7. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
   URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
   — Bridged foresight and design through experiential futures: making futures tangible through designed experiences.

8. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
   DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
   — Detailed case study where foresight methods (scenario development) meet design practice (experiential staging).

9. **Candy, S., & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3–22.
   DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
   — Combining foresight with ethnographic methods; futures practice grounded in lived experience.

10. **Fry, T. (2009). Design Futuring: Sustainability, Ethics and New Practice.** Berg Publishers.
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474293488
    — "Design futuring" and "defuturing": design is central to whether futures are sustained or destroyed. Bridges foresight's analytical stance with design's material agency.

11. **Willis, A.-M. (2006). Ontological Designing.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 4(2), 69–92.
    DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514
    — "Design designs us back" (ontological designing); the futural dimension of all design. Theoretical bridge between foresight and design practice.

## Design Foresight as Integrated Practice

12. **Irwin, T. (2015). Transition Design: A Proposal for a New Area of Design Practice, Study, and Research.** *Design and Culture*, 7(2), 229–246.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829
    — Transition design uses foresight methods (backcasting, visioning) as core components of design for societal transitions.

13. **Irwin, T., Kossoff, G., & Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Transition Design Provocation.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 3–11.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085681
    — Foundational provocation establishing Transition Design as a distinct field combining foresight, systems thinking, and design.

14. **Mazé, R. (2019). Politics of Designing Visions of the Future.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 23–38.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0003
    — Analyzed the political dimensions of design-led futuring; who gets to envision whose future. Critical intervention at foresight-design intersection.

15. **Buehring, J., & Bishop, P. C. (2020). Foresight and Design: New Support for Strategic Decision Making.** *She Ji: The Journal of Design, Economics, and Innovation*, 6(3), 408–432.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.sheji.2020.07.002
    — Systematic integration of foresight and design as a new approach to strategic decision-making; maps the emerging field.

16. **Miller, R. (Ed.) (2018). Transforming the Future: Anticipation in the 21st Century.** Routledge / UNESCO.
    DOI: 10.4324/9781351048002
    — Futures Literacy framework; anticipation as a human capacity that can be developed. Includes Futures Literacy Laboratories methodology.

17. **Bishop, P. C., & Hines, A. (2012). Teaching about the Future: The Basics of Foresight Education.** Palgrave Macmillan.
    DOI: 10.1057/9781137020703
    — Comprehensive foresight education framework; codifies teaching methods for futures studies applicable to design education.

18. **Hines, A., & Bishop, P. (2015). Thinking about the Future: Guidelines for Strategic Foresight (2nd ed.).** Hinesight.
    ISBN: 978-0-9896369-3-3
    — Practitioner guidelines for strategic foresight; distills expertise from three dozen senior foresight professionals.

## Scenario Methods in Design

19. **Ilstedt, S., & Wangel, J. (2014). Altering Expectations: How Design Fictions and Backcasting Can Leverage Sustainable Lifestyles.** *Proceedings of DRS '14*.
    DOI: 10.21606/drs.2014.17
    — Explicit integration of backcasting (foresight method) with design fiction for sustainable futures.

20. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
    — The PPPP futures cone adapted from Voros (2003); foresight's conceptual framework entering design through Speculative Everything.

21. **Gaziulusoy, A. İ., & Erdoğan Öztekin, E. (2019). Design for Sustainability Transitions: Origins, Attitudes and Future Directions.** *Sustainability*, 11(13), 3601.
    DOI: 10.3390/su11133601
    — Systematic mapping of design for sustainability transitions; foresight methods as tools for transition design.

22. **Cuhls, K. E. (2020). Horizon Scanning in Foresight — Why Horizon Scanning Is Only a Part of the Game.** *Futures & Foresight Science*, 2(1), e23.
    DOI: 10.1002/ffo2.23
    — Clarified horizon scanning within foresight; relevant to how design researchers use foresight methods.

## Critical Perspectives on Futures

23. **Appadurai, A. (2013). The Future as Cultural Fact: Essays on the Global Condition.** Verso Books.
    ISBN: 978-1-84467-985-5
    — The capacity to aspire is unequally distributed; relevant to justice in design foresight.

24. **Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Just Design: Being Dogmatic about Defining Speculative Critical Design Fiction Futures.** Medium.
    URL: https://medium.com/@camerontw/just-design-b1f97cb3996f
    — Attempted to clarify distinctions between Design Futures, Design Fiction, Speculative Design, and Critical Design; argued all must be part of professional practice.

25. **Schultz, T., Abdulla, D., Ansari, A., Canlı, E., Keshavarz, M., Kiem, M., Prado de O. Martins, L., & Vieira de Oliveira, P. J. S. (2018). What Is at Stake with Decolonizing Design? A Roundtable.** *Design and Culture*, 10(1), 81–101.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2018.1434368
    — Whose futures? Decolonizing critique of Western-centric futuring in design.

26. **Escobar, A. (2018). Designs for the Pluriverse: Radical Interdependence, Autonomy, and the Making of Worlds.** Duke University Press.
    DOI: 10.1215/9780822371816
    — Decolonial design futures; multiple worldings rather than a single foresight frame. "Autonomous design" from communal perspectives.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **Speculative Design**: Foresight provides the analytical framework (scenarios, cones) that Speculative Design materializes (Dunne & Raby 2013 ← Voros 2003)
- **Design Fiction**: Foresight scenarios become Design Fiction worlds (Ilstedt & Wangel 2014)
- **World Building**: Foresight scenarios provide the factual grounding for fictional world construction (Candy 2010)
- **Transition Design**: Uses foresight methods (backcasting, visioning) as core practice (Irwin et al. 2015)
- **Experiential Futures**: The primary bridge between foresight and design (Candy & Dunagan 2017)
- **Afrofuturism / Decolonial Design**: Challenges whose futures foresight anticipates (Appadurai 2013; Schultz et al. 2018)
- **More-than-Human Design**: Expands the temporal horizon of foresight beyond human actors

## Summary

Foresight brings systematic futures methods — horizon scanning, scenario planning, backcasting, Causal Layered Analysis — to design research. The discipline has its own genealogy (Dator's Manoa School, Bell's foundations, Slaughter's foresight principle) and methodological frameworks (Voros's futures cone, Inayatullah's CLA, Schwartz's scenario planning) that predate their uptake in design.

The intersection emerged through multiple channels: Candy's experiential futures (2010) bridged foresight and design by making scenarios inhabitable; Fry's design futuring (2009) argued design itself is a futural practice; Irwin's transition design (2015) made foresight methods core to designing for societal transitions. Buehring & Bishop (2020) mapped this integration systematically, while Miller's Futures Literacy framework (2018) expanded anticipation from a professional skill to a universal human capacity.

Critical perspectives from Mazé (2019), Appadurai (2013), and the decolonizing design roundtable (Schultz et al. 2018) interrogate the politics of who gets to envision the future, connecting foresight to justice concerns. The Dunne & Raby PPPP cone — adapted from Voros's (2003) foresight framework — exemplifies how foresight concepts have been absorbed into design vocabulary, often losing their analytical rigour in translation (Tonkinwise 2015).
