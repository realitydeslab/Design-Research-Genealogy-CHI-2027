# Observatory-Informed Lineage Map

## Source
Design Research Observatory: https://observatory.designresearch.works/
Run by Joe Lindley & Paul Coulton, Imagination Lancaster, Lancaster University.

The Observatory defines 21 key design research concepts with cross-links showing lineage relationships. This document maps those relationships as the skeleton for our genealogy.

---

## The 21 Concepts

1. **Research through Design (RtD)** — "A research method and a theory of knowledge (epistemology)" based on learning through design processes. Related: Practice-based research, Constructive design research.

2. **Speculative Design** — "An approach that aims to provoke thoughts and discussion about potential technological, social, or cultural developments rather than immediately trying to solve problems." Parent: RtD. Related: Critical Design, Design Fiction, Material Speculation.

3. **Critical Design** — "An approach to RtD that uses the process of designing to challenge societal norms, assumptions, and trajectories." Unlike traditional design (solutions), Critical Design asks questions. Parent: RtD.

4. **Design Fiction** — "A type of Speculative Design that takes inspiration from science fiction." Creates fictional/story worlds and places prototype designs within them. Parent: Speculative Design.

5. **More-than-Human Design** — "An approach that deliberately moves beyond Human-Centred Design to consider interconnected relationships between people, technology, and the environment." Draws on posthumanism, new materialism, ANT. Contains: Thing Ethnography. Responds to: HCD.

6. **Participatory Design (PD)** — "A collaborative approach that actively involves people affected by a design in the process." Related: Co-Design (sometimes used interchangeably).

7. **Co-Design** — "Bringing together lived experiences of communities and professional expertise in collaborative design processes." Related: PD, but emphasizes direct inclusion in decision-making.

8. **Thing Ethnography** — "An unusual approach to ethnography that tries to imagine the perspective of objects and devices." A practical way to do More-than-Human Design. Parent: Ethnography, More-than-Human Design.

9. **Anticipatory Ethnography** — "A hybrid approach that combines Design Fiction with Ethnography to study possible futures by conducting ethnographic analysis of design fictions." Parents: Design Fiction + Ethnography.

10. **Constructionism** — "A theory of learning that says people learn most effectively when they actively construct something." Explains how Design Research generates knowledge through making. Epistemological foundation for RtD.

11. **Slow Technology** — "An approach to designing technological systems that prioritises reflection and calmness over speed and efficiency." Emerged from Slow Design movement. Related: RtD.

12. **World Building** — "The practice of creating internally-consistent imaginary worlds by drawing on reality, history, and research while building on that through creativity and speculation." Related: Design Fiction.

13. **Foresight** — "A discipline focused on anticipating possible futures to inform present-day actions and strategic decisions." Techniques: Horizon Scanning, Trend Analysis, Backcasting. Related: Speculative Design, Design Fiction.

14. **Ethnography** — "A research approach where researchers immerse themselves in situations and contexts they want to understand." Related: Co-Design, PD, Speculative Design, Thing Ethnography, Anticipatory Ethnography.

15. **Human-Centered Design (HCD)** — "An approach to creating products and services that begins by trying to understand people's goals, abilities, and environments." Synonymous with User-Centred Design. Related: PD. Challenged by: More-than-Human Design.

16. **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)** — "A multidisciplinary field bringing together computer science, design, psychology to create usable computer systems." Significant overlap with Design Research. Related: RtD, Design Fiction, User Research.

17. **Afrofuturism** — "A multifaceted concept that applies and reflects the experiences of people from the African diaspora to speculation and futures thinking." A form of Speculative Design with empathic engagement with African diaspora aesthetics, politics, experiences. Parent: Speculative Design.

18. **Material Speculation** — "An approach to RtD complementary to Speculative Design, Design Fiction and Critical Design but focusing on functional prototypes deployed in real-world settings." Parents: RtD + Speculative Design.

19. **Prototyping** — "The process of creating an early sample, model, or preliminary version of a product, system or concept." In design research: generates insights and knowledge (not just iteration).

20. **User Research** — "The practice of learning about the people who will use a product or service." Key part of HCD. Related: Ethnography, HCD.

21. **IoT (Internet of Things)** — Application domain. Growth in 2000s-2010s drove demand for future-focused design research. Connected to Design Fiction, Thing-centered design, Material Speculation.


### Cross-Cutting Connections
1. **RtD ← Constructionism**: Constructionism provides the epistemological justification for RtD's claim that making = knowledge production
2. **HCD → More-than-Human Design**: MtHD emerged as a direct response to HCD's anthropocentrism
3. **Ethnography → Thing Ethnography → More-than-Human Design**: Ethnographic methods adapted for nonhuman perspectives
4. **Design Fiction × Ethnography → Anticipatory Ethnography**: Hybrid method for studying possible futures ethnographically
5. **Speculative Design → Afrofuturism**: Decolonial expansion of speculative practice
6. **Foresight → Speculative Design / Design Fiction**: Professional futures discipline informing design speculation
7. **PD ↔ Ethnography ↔ Co-Design**: Cluster of participatory/observational methods
8. **Material Speculation bridges RtD + Speculative Design**: Functional prototypes in real settings (vs. gallery objects)
9. **IoT as catalyst**: Growth of connected devices drove need for Design Fiction, Thing Ethnography, Material Speculation

### Key Insight: The Observatory's Implicit Genealogy

The Observatory structures its 21 concepts into an implicit genealogy:

**Epistemological Layer:**
- Constructionism (making = learning = knowledge) → grounds RtD

**Methodological Core:**
- RtD → the central methodology from which speculative, critical, and material approaches branch

**Speculative Branch:**
- RtD → Speculative Design → Design Fiction → World Building
- RtD → Speculative Design → Afrofuturism (decolonial branch)
- RtD → Critical Design
- RtD + Speculative Design → Material Speculation

**Participatory Branch:**
- PD ↔ Co-Design (Scandinavian roots, expanded)

**Observational Branch:**
- Ethnography → Thing Ethnography (nonhuman perspectives)
- Design Fiction + Ethnography → Anticipatory Ethnography (futures observation)

**Temporal/Reflective Branch:**
- Slow Technology (temporal design, reflection)
- Foresight (futures discipline)

**Contextual Layer:**
- HCD (the dominant paradigm being challenged)
- HCI (the disciplinary home for much design research)
- User Research (the empirical practice within HCD)
- IoT (the application domain driving new methods)

**Paradigm Shift:**
- HCD → [challenged by] → More-than-Human Design → Thing Ethnography

## What the Observatory Adds to Our Original Draft

### Lineages We MISSED:
1. **Anticipatory Ethnography** — Design Fiction × Ethnography hybrid (Lindley, Coulton)
2. **Thing Ethnography** — Practical entry point for More-than-Human Design (Giaccardi)
3. **Constructionism** — Epistemological foundation for RtD (Papert)
4. **Slow Technology** — Temporal/reflective design (Hallnäs & Redström)
5. **World Building** — Distinct from but related to Design Fiction (Coulton, Lindley)
6. **Foresight** — Professional futures discipline meeting design
7. **Afrofuturism** — Decolonial speculative design
8. **Material Speculation** — Functional prototypes for critical inquiry (Wakkary)
9. **HCD → Posthuman trajectory** — The origin point More-than-Human responds to
10. **Ethnography in Design** — How ethnography entered HCI/design research
11. **Prototyping as Research** — Knowledge production through making (not just iteration)

### Framing DIFFERENCES from our draft:
1. **RtD as the central hub**: Observatory places RtD at the center of everything, more than our draft did
2. **Constructionism as epistemological ground**: We had no epistemological layer; the Observatory provides one
3. **Material Speculation as distinct**: We mentioned Wakkary et al. under More-than-Human; Observatory treats it as a separate methodology bridging RtD and Speculative Design
4. **HCD as explicit origin**: Observatory makes the HCD → More-than-Human trajectory explicit, which we only implied
5. **Ethnography as a root method**: Observatory treats ethnography as foundational, branching into Thing Ethnography and Anticipatory Ethnography — we didn't treat it as a separate lineage
6. **Prototyping as epistemology**: Observatory distinguishes commercial prototyping from research prototyping (knowledge generation)
