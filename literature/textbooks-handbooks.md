# Literature Review: Textbooks & Handbooks in Design Research Methods

## Overview

Textbooks and handbooks serve as genealogical markers — they signal when a methodology has matured from individual papers into a codified, teachable discipline. This file tracks both foundational texts and recent (2022–2026) codification activity. The 2022–2026 period shows an extraordinary burst of handbook production, particularly in participatory design, more-than-human design, speculative design, and soma design — confirming these as established methodological lineages.

---

## Foundational Textbooks (Pre-2020)

### Design Research Methods — General

1. **Laurel, B. (Ed.) (2003). *Design Research: Methods and Perspectives.*** MIT Press. ISBN 9780262122634.
   - Early anthology establishing design research as a legitimate field. Contributors include Buchanan, Margolin, Frayling-era thinkers.

2. **Blessing, L. T. M., & Chakrabarti, A. (2009). *DRM, a Design Research Methodology.*** Springer. ISBN 978-1-84882-587-1.
   - Formalized methodology for conducting design research. Influential in engineering design research tradition.

3. **Koskinen, I., Zimmerman, J., Binder, T., Redström, J., & Wensveen, S. (2011). *Design Research Through Practice: From the Lab, Field, and Showroom.*** Morgan Kaufmann. ISBN 9780123855022.
   - The canonical constructive design research textbook. Introduced the lab/field/showroom taxonomy that structures our genealogy's Layer 7.

4. **Olson, J. S., & Kellogg, W. A. (Eds.) (2014). *Ways of Knowing in HCI.*** Springer. ISBN 9781493903771.
   - Epistemological foundations for HCI research. Frames the "ways of knowing" question that intermediate-level knowledge debates later address.

### HCI Research Methods

5. **Cairns, P., & Cox, A. L. (Eds.) (2008). *Research Methods for Human-Computer Interaction.*** Cambridge University Press. ISBN 9780521690317.

6. **Lazar, J., Feng, J. H., & Hochheiser, H. (2017). *Research Methods in Human-Computer Interaction*, 2nd ed.** Morgan Kaufmann. ISBN 9780128053904.
   - The standard HCI methods textbook. Covers surveys, experiments, diary studies, and content analysis — the quantitative/qualitative methods that RtD positions itself against/alongside.

### Speculative & Critical Design

7. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). *Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.*** MIT Press. ISBN 9780262019842.
   - The codification moment for speculative/critical design. Introduced the PPPP futures cone to a design audience.

### Participatory Design — First Edition

8. **Simonsen, J., & Robertson, T. (Eds.) (2013). *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design.*** Routledge. ISBN 9780415720212.
   - First comprehensive PD handbook. Established PD as a field with its own methods, ethics, and history.

## Recent Codification (2020–2026)

### Design Research Methods — General

9. **Muratovski, G. (2022). *Research for Designers: A Guide to Methods and Practice*, 2nd ed.** SAGE. ISBN 9781529708158.
   - Updated introductory textbook for design research. Widely used in design education.

10. **Koskinen, I. (2020). *Drifting by Intention: Four Epistemic Traditions from within Constructive Design Research.*** Springer. ISBN 978-3-030-37896-7.
    - Follow-up to the 2011 textbook. Refines the epistemological framing: four traditions (art-based, critical, participatory, experimental).

11. **Joost, G., Christoph, A., & Paim, B. (Eds.) (2023). *Research Handbook on Design Thinking.*** Edward Elgar. ISBN 9781802203127.
    - Design thinking codified as research methodology, not just industry practice.

12. **Rodgers, P. A., & Yee, J. (Eds.) (2024). *The Routledge Companion to Design Research*, 2nd ed.** Routledge.
    - Major updated companion. Covers the full landscape of design research methods.

13. **Gibson, M. R., & Owens, K. M. (Eds.) (2026). *Navigating the Landscapes of Design Research: Finding Your Way.*** Routledge (Design Research for Change series).
    - Latest entry. "Finding your way" framing acknowledges the field's complexity.

### Participatory Design — New Editions

14. **Bødker, S., Dindler, C., Iversen, O. S., & Smith, R. C. (2022). *Participatory Design.*** Springer (Synthesis Lectures on Human-Centered Informatics).
    - PD codified as a Synthesis Lecture — concise, teachable format. Signals PD's textbook status.

15. **Smith, R. C., Loi, D., Winschiers-Theophilus, H., Huybrechts, L., & Simonsen, J. (Eds.) (2025). *Routledge International Handbook of Contemporary Participatory Design.*** Routledge. ISBN 9781032368887.
    - New edition of the PD handbook. "Contemporary" signals updated scope including agonistic PD, infrastructuring, participatory AI, and more-than-human PD.

### More-than-Human Design

16. **Tironi, M., Chilet, M., Ureta Marín, C., & Hermansen, P. (Eds.) (2023). *Design for More-Than-Human Futures: Towards Post-Anthropocentric Worlding.*** Routledge.
    - First book-length treatment of more-than-human design futures. "Post-anthropocentric worlding" as framing.

17. **Poikolainen Rosén, A., Salovaara, A., Botero, A., & Søndergaard, M. L. J. (Eds.) (2024). *More-Than-Human Design in Practice.*** Routledge. ISBN 9781032741208.
    - "In practice" — the theory-to-method transition codified. Companion to their 2025 *Interactions* forum piece.

### Speculative & Futures Design

18. **Wakkary, R., & Oogjes, D. (2024). *The Importance of Speculation in Design Research.*** Springer (Synthesis Lectures on Human-Centered Informatics).
    - Speculation codified as Synthesis Lecture. From Wakkary, who also authored *Things We Could Design* (2021).

19. **SpeculativeEdu Consortium (2024). *Beyond Speculative Design: Past — Present — Future.*** Open-access (Erasmus+ SpeculativeEdu project).
    - Open textbook for teaching speculative design. "Beyond" signals the field's post-Dunne & Raby evolution.

20. **Mandir, E., & Gross, B. (2024). *Designing Futures: Speculation, Critique, Innovation.*** Laurence King.
    - Futures-oriented design textbook bridging speculation and innovation.

### Soma Design & Embodiment

21. **Höök, K. (2018/2024 paperback reissue). *Designing with the Body: Somaesthetic Interaction Design.*** MIT Press.
    - The soma design textbook. 2024 paperback reissue in MIT Press "Design Thinking, Design Theory" series signals canonical status.

### Service Design

22. **Penin, L., Prendiville, A., & Sangiorgi, D. (Eds.) (2026). *The Bloomsbury Handbook of Service Design: Plural Perspectives and a Critical Contemporary Agenda.*** Bloomsbury Visual Arts.
    - Service design codified. Adjacent to our genealogy — service design draws on PD, co-design, and RtD.

### Updated Foundational Texts (Re-codification)

23. **Cross, N. (2023). *Design Thinking: Understanding How Designers Think and Work*, 2nd ed.** Bloomsbury Visual Arts.
    - Cross updating his own foundational work. "Designerly ways of knowing" re-codified for a new generation.

24. **Cross, N. (2025). *Designerly Ways of Knowing and Thinking*, 2nd ed.** Springer.
    - Direct update of the 1982/2001 "designerly ways of knowing" thesis. The design methods movement's key concept, now in textbook form.

25. **Binder, T., De Michelis, G., Ehn, P., Jacucci, G., Linde, P., & Wagner, I. (2025 paperback reissue). *Design Things.*** MIT Press.
    - Ehn's "design things" concept — foundational for agonistic PD — reissued. Canonical status confirmed.

26. **Dorst, K. (2024 paperback reissue). *Frame Innovation: Create New Thinking by Design.*** MIT Press.
    - Frame creation as design method. Reissue signals ongoing relevance.

### General Research Methods (Used in Design Programs)

27. **Creswell, J. W., & Creswell, J. D. (2023). *Research Design: Qualitative, Quantitative, and Mixed Methods Approaches*, 6th ed.** SAGE. ISBN 9781071817940.
    - The standard research design textbook. Not design-specific, but foundational for positioning design research within broader research methodology.

28. **Chu, H. (2024). *Research Methods and Design Beyond a Single Discipline: From Principles to Practice.*** Routledge. ISBN 9780367898854.
    - Interdisciplinary methods — relevant for design research's boundary-crossing nature.

## Genealogical Significance

### Codification Timeline

The textbook record reveals a clear codification sequence:

| Period | What Gets Codified | Key Texts |
|---|---|---|
| 2003–2011 | Design research as a field | Laurel (2003), Blessing (2009), Koskinen et al. (2011) |
| 2013 | Speculative design, PD | Dunne & Raby (2013), Simonsen & Robertson (2013) |
| 2017–2018 | HCI methods, soma design | Lazar et al. (2017), Höök (2018) |
| 2020–2022 | Constructive DR refinement, PD as lecture | Koskinen (2020), Bødker et al. (2022) |
| 2023–2024 | More-than-human, speculation, design thinking | Tironi et al. (2023), Wakkary & Oogjes (2024), Rodgers & Yee (2024) |
| 2025–2026 | PD new edition, Cross updates, service design | Smith et al. (2025), Cross (2025), Penin et al. (2026) |

### Key Pattern: The 2022–2026 Codification Burst

The density of handbook production in 2022–2026 is unprecedented. At least **12 major handbooks/textbooks** in four years, compared to roughly 8 in the preceding decade. This signals:

1. **Field maturation:** Methods that were experimental in 2010–2015 are now teachable
2. **Institutional demand:** Design PhD programs need textbooks
3. **The Synthesis Lectures vehicle:** Springer's series (Bødker et al. 2022, Wakkary & Oogjes 2024) provides a standardized format for codifying design methods
4. **Re-codification cycle:** Cross (2023, 2025), Ehn (2025 reissue), Höök (2024 reissue) — foundational figures updating their own codifications

### Connection to Self-Codification Trend

This textbook burst parallels the self-codification wave identified in `recent-trends-2024-2026.md` — the field is simultaneously reviewing itself (systematic reviews) and codifying itself (textbooks). Both activities signal a transition from methodological invention to methodological consolidation.
