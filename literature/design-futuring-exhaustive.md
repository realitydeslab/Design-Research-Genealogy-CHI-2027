# Design Futuring: Exhaustive Literature Map

## Overview

This file reconstructs and expands the corpus of Celik & Kaya (2025), who reviewed 117 papers in "Reviewing the expansion in Design Futuring Research: Emergent concepts around Speculative Design and Design Fiction" (*Futures*, DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2025.103615). Since their full reference list is behind an Elsevier paywall, we reconstructed and expanded their corpus through intensive multi-query searching across ACM DL, Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar, Brave Search, and publisher databases.

**Search methodology:** 40+ distinct queries across 7 query sets covering design futuring core, speculative design (systematic reviews, applications by domain), design fiction (methods, forms, evaluation), futures studies × design, emergent concepts, recent papers (2023–2026), and key researchers. Expansion round 2 (April 2026) added 40 papers via intensive web searches targeting DRS 2024, CHI 2025–2026, DIS 2024–2025, Futures journal 2023–2025, and key researcher publication pages.

**Total papers catalogued:** 160

## 1. Design Futuring Core (Fry, Willis, Ontological Design)

1. **Fry, T. (1999). A New Design Philosophy: An Introduction to Defuturing.** UNSW Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-86840-753-0 | URL: https://www.bloomsburycollections.com/book/defuturing-a-new-design-philosophy/
   - Introduced "defuturing" — the way design destroys futures through unsustainable practices. Foundational text for design futuring.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Transition design, sustainability.

2. **Fry, T. (2009). Design Futuring: Sustainability, Ethics and New Practice.** Berg Publishers.
   DOI: 10.5040/9781474293488
   - Introduced "design futuring" as a concept; argued design is central to whether futures are sustained or destroyed.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Transition design, ontological design.

3. **Fry, T. (2019). Starting at the End: A Provocation for Redirective Practice.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(4), 159–162.
   URL: https://jfsdigital.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/18-Fry-Starting_at_the_End.pdf
   - Late-career provocation on redirective practice — designing away from defuturing.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Transition design, redirective practice.

4. **Willis, A.-M. (2006). Ontological Designing.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 4(2), 69–92.
   DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514
   - Proposed "ontological designing" — design designs us back. Foundational for understanding design's futural dimension.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Transition design, posthumanism.

5. **Tonkinwise, C. (2014). Design Away.** In T. Fry (Ed.), *Design and the Question of History*, Bloomsbury, 198–213.
   DOI: 10.5040/9781474293792.ch-013
   - Argued design must "design away" unsustainable practices; connected to defuturing and transition.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Transition design, sustainability.

6. **Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Just Design: Being Dogmatic about Defining Speculative Critical Design Future Fiction.** Online essay.
   URL: https://medium.com/@camerontw/just-design-b1f97cb3996f
   - Influential taxonomy of distinctions between speculative, critical, and discursive design.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring / speculative design theory. *Connections:* Critical design.

7. **Yu, S. (2025). Transition Designing as Ontological Redirection: Key Principles for Transition-Oriented Futuring.** *World Futures Review*.
   DOI: 10.1177/19467567251338771
   - Seven principles for transition-oriented futuring grounded in ontological design theory.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core / transition design. *Connections:* Ontological design, redirective practice.

8. **Kozubaev, S., Elsden, C., Howell, N., Søndergaard, M. L. J., Merrill, N., Schulte, B., & Wong, R. Y. (2020). Expanding Modes of Reflection in Design Futuring.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376526
   - Identified five reflective modes for design futuring: designerly form-giving, temporality, positionality, real-world engagement, knowledge production.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring meta-theory. *Connections:* RtD, speculative design, design fiction.

9. **Mazé, R. (2019). Politics of Designing Visions of the Future.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 23–38.
   DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0003
   - Political dimensions of design-led futuring; who gets to envision whose future.
   - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core. *Connections:* Participatory design, decolonial design.

10. **Gonzatto, R. F., van Amstel, F. M. C., Merkle, L. E., & Hartmann, T. (2013). The Ideology of the Future in Design Fictions.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 36–45.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.772524
    - Critical analysis of ideological assumptions embedded in design fiction and futuring practices.
    - *Sub-domain:* Design futuring core / design fiction. *Connections:* Critical theory, ideology.

## 2. Speculative Design — Foundational & Theoretical

11. **Dunne, A. (1999). Hertzian Tales: Electronic Products, Aesthetic Experience, and Critical Design.** RCA CRD / MIT Press (2005 reissue).
    ISBN: 978-0-262-54178-7 | URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262541787/hertzian-tales/
    - Origin text for critical design. Proposed design as medium for cultural commentary on electronic products.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative/critical design foundational. *Connections:* RtD, critical theory.

12. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2001). Design Noir: The Secret Life of Electronic Objects.** Birkhäuser.
    ISBN: 978-3-7643-6566-2
    - Extended critical design through speculative product proposals exploring psychological dimensions of technology.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design foundational. *Connections:* Critical design, electronic objects.

13. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1 | URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/speculative-everything/
    - Definitive statement of speculative design. Introduced the PPPP cone of futures.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design foundational. *Connections:* Design fiction, futures studies.

14. **Malpass, M. (2017). Critical Design in Context: History, Theory, and Practices.** Bloomsbury.
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474241991
    - First book-length scholarly analysis of critical design; distinguished associative, speculative, and critical strands.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative/critical design theory. *Connections:* Design history.

15. **Auger, J. (2013). Speculative Design: Crafting the Speculation.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 11–35.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.767276
    - Analyzed speculative design methods and their effectiveness; proposed craft of speculation.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design practice. *Connections:* RtD, design methods.

16. **Bardzell, J., & Bardzell, S. (2013). What is "Critical" about Critical Design?** *Proceedings of CHI '13*, 3297–3306.
    DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451
    - Analyzed theoretical commitments of critical design from a humanities perspective.
    - *Sub-domain:* Critical/speculative design theory. *Connections:* Critical theory, HCI.

17. **Pierce, J., Sengers, P., Hirsch, T., Jenkins, T., Gaver, W., & DiSalvo, C. (2015). Expanding and Refining Design and Criticality in HCI.** *Proceedings of CHI '15*, 2083–2092.
    DOI: 10.1145/2702123.2702438
    - Panel paper expanding design criticality in HCI beyond Dunne & Raby.
    - *Sub-domain:* Critical/speculative design theory. *Connections:* RtD, adversarial design.

18. **Pierce, J. (2021). In Tension with Progression: Grasping the Frictional Tendencies of Speculative, Critical, and other Alternative Designs.** *Proceedings of CHI '21*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3411764.3445406
    - Major theoretical paper. Develops progressional vs. frictional design; 5 frictional tendencies. First comprehensive unifying theory.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design theory. *Connections:* RtD, critical design, adversarial design.

19. **Wong, R. Y., & Khovanskaya, V. (2018). Speculative Design in HCI: From Corporate Imaginations to Critical Orientations.** In *New Directions in Third Wave HCI*, Springer, 175–202.
    DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73356-2_11
    - Mapped spectrum of speculative design in HCI from corporate to critical orientations.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design theory. *Connections:* STS, critical HCI.

20. **Prado de O. Martins, L., & Oliveira, P. (2014). Questioning the "Critical" in Speculative & Critical Design.** Medium essay.
    URL: https://medium.com/a-parede/questioning-the-critical-in-speculative-critical-design-5a355cac2ca4
    - Prominent critique of SCD's Eurocentrism, privilege, and limited engagement with marginalized perspectives.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design critique. *Connections:* Decolonial design, feminist HCI.

21. **Michael, M. (2012). "What Are We Busy Doing?": Engaging the Idiot.** *Science, Technology, & Human Values*, 37(5), 528–554.
    DOI: 10.1177/0162243911428624
    - Introduced the "idiot" as a figure for speculative thought in STS/design.
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design theory. *Connections:* STS, philosophy.

22. **Tharp, B. M., & Tharp, S. M. (2019). Discursive Design: Critical, Speculative, and Alternative Things.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-03898-2 | URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262038980/discursive-design/
    - Proposed discursive design as unifying field for socially minded design practice beyond critical/speculative/adversarial divisions.
    - *Sub-domain:* Discursive design. *Connections:* Critical design, speculative design.

23. **DiSalvo, C. (2012). Adversarial Design.** MIT Press.
    DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/8732.001.0001
    - Proposed adversarial design as political practice; artifacts as means of agonistic contestation.
    - *Sub-domain:* Adversarial/critical design. *Connections:* Political theory, speculative design.

24. **Galloway, A., & Caudwell, C. (2019). Speculative Design as Research Method.** In *Undesign*, Routledge, 85–96.
    DOI: 10.4324/9781315526379-8
    - Positioned speculative design as research methodology using Haraway's "staying with the trouble."
    - *Sub-domain:* Speculative design methodology. *Connections:* RtD, feminist theory.
