# Literature Review: Futuring Methods in Design

## Overview

Futuring methods in design draw from futures studies, foresight, and transition research. Key lineages include transition design (Irwin, Kossoff, Tonkinwise at CMU), design futuring and defuturing (Fry, Willis), experiential futures (Candy, Dunagan), speculative and critical design (Dunne & Raby), design fiction (Sterling, Bleecker, Lindley & Coulton), participatory futures (Mazé, Light, Akama), decolonial futures (Escobar, Ansari, Schultz et al.), design for policy (Kimbell, Mazé), and futures studies methods adapted for design (Voros, Inayatullah, Scupelli). These approaches position design as a vehicle for imagining and navigating long-term systemic change.

This review targets the corpus identified by Celik & Kaya (2025), who reviewed 117 papers on design futuring (DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2025.103615), and substantially expands it.

## 1. Futures Studies Foundations

1. **Dator, J. (2009). Alternative Futures at the Manoa School.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 14(2), 1–18.
   URL: https://jfsdigital.org/articles-and-essays/2009-2/vol-14-no-2-november/articles/alternative-futures-at-the-manoa-school/
   - Overview of the Manoa School; Dator's four generic futures images (continuation, collapse, discipline, transformation).

2. **Slaughter, R. A. (1995). The Foresight Principle: Cultural Recovery in the 21st Century.** Praeger.
   ISBN: 978-0-275-95259-0
   - Foundational foresight text; argued foresight is a necessary capacity for navigating complexity.

3. **Bell, W. (1997). Foundations of Futures Studies: Human Science for a New Era, Volume 1.** Transaction Publishers.
   ISBN: 978-1-56000-271-4
   - Comprehensive philosophical and methodological foundations for futures studies.

4. **Inayatullah, S. (1998). Causal Layered Analysis: Poststructuralism as Method.** *Futures*, 30(8), 815–829.
   DOI: 10.1016/S0016-3287(98)00086-X
   - Introduced Causal Layered Analysis (CLA) — a layered futures methodology moving from litany to metaphor/myth.

5. **Voros, J. (2003). A Generic Foresight Process Framework.** *Foresight*, 5(3), 10–21.
   DOI: 10.1108/14636680310698379
   - Widely used framework for foresight processes; introduced the futures cone taxonomy (possible, plausible, probable, preferable).

6. **Schwartz, P. (1991). The Art of the Long View: Planning for the Future in an Uncertain World.** Doubleday.
   ISBN: 978-0-385-26732-5
   - Classic on scenario planning; widely used in design-led futuring.

7. **Inayatullah, S. (2004). The Causal Layered Analysis (CLA) Reader: Theory and Case Studies of an Integrative and Transformative Methodology.** Tamkang University Press.
   ISBN: 978-957-01-6414-7
   - Collected CLA case studies and theoretical elaborations across domains.

8. **Voros, J. (2017). Big History and Anticipation: Using Big History as a Framework for Global Foresight.** In R. Poli (Ed.), *Handbook of Anticipation*. Springer.
   DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-31737-3_95-1
   - Extended the futures cone framework; clarified its use and history in design contexts.

9. **Adam, B., & Groves, C. (2007). Future Matters: Action, Knowledge, Ethics.** Brill.
   ISBN: 978-90-04-16177-0 | DOI: 10.1163/ej.9789004161771.i-218
   - Sociological analysis of how futures are constructed; foundational for design's engagement with temporality.

10. **Appadurai, A. (2013). The Future as Cultural Fact: Essays on the Global Condition.** Verso Books.
    ISBN: 978-1-84467-985-5
    - Argued that the capacity to aspire is unequally distributed; relevant to justice in design futuring.

## 2. Design Futuring & Defuturing (Fry, Willis)

11. **Fry, T. (1999). A New Design Philosophy: An Introduction to Defuturing.** UNSW Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-86840-753-0
    URL: https://www.bloomsburycollections.com/book/defuturing-a-new-design-philosophy/
    - Introduced "defuturing" — the way design destroys futures through unsustainable practices.

12. **Fry, T. (2009). Design Futuring: Sustainability, Ethics and New Practice.** Berg/Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-84788-244-3 | DOI: 10.5040/9781474293488
    - Foundational text coining "design futuring"; argued design is central to whether futures are sustained or destroyed.

13. **Fry, T. (2011). Design as Politics.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-84788-568-0 | DOI: 10.5040/9781474293525
    - Extended defuturing analysis to political design; design as a mode of governing futures.

14. **Fry, T. (2012). Becoming Human by Design.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-0-85785-355-4 | DOI: 10.5040/9781474293556
    - Argued that the human is constituted through design; design as ontological becoming.

15. **Fry, T. (2017). Remaking Cities: An Introduction to Urban Metrofitting.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-4742-3694-4
    - Applied design futuring to urban contexts; cities as sites of futural design intervention.

16. **Fry, T. (2019). Starting at the End: A Counter-Narrative for Design.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(4).
    URL: https://jfsdigital.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/18-Fry-Starting_at_the_End.pdf
    - Proposed starting design from the endpoint of sustainability rather than from present conditions.

17. **Willis, A.-M. (2006). Ontological Designing.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 4(2), 69–92.
    DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514
    - Proposed that design designs us back ("ontological designing"); foundational for understanding design's futural dimension.

18. **Willis, A.-M. (Ed.) (2019). The Design Philosophy Reader.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-350-06518-9
    - Collected key texts in design philosophy including ontological design and futuring perspectives.

## 3. Transition Design (Irwin, Tonkinwise, Kossoff)

19. **Irwin, T. (2015). Transition Design: A Proposal for a New Area of Design Practice, Study, and Research.** *Design and Culture*, 7(2), 229–246.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829
    - Foundational paper proposing transition design as a new design discipline focused on societal transitions toward sustainability.

20. **Irwin, T., Kossoff, G., & Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Transition Design Provocation.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 3–11.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085688
    - Laid out the provocative vision for transition design as a field integrating futures, systems, and place.

21. **Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Design for Transitions — From and To What?** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 85–92.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085686
    - Critically examined what transition design transitions from and toward; questioned the "from" as much as the "to."

22. **Kossoff, G. (2015). Holism and the Reconstitution of Everyday Life: A Framework for Transition to a Sustainable Society.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 25–38.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085690
    - Theoretical grounding for transition design in holistic thinking, nested systems, and everyday life.

23. **Irwin, T. (2018). The Emerging Transition Design Approach.** *Proceedings of DRS '18*, 968–980.
    DOI: 10.21606/drs.2018.210
    - Updated framing of transition design incorporating systems thinking, stakeholder relations, and new ways of designing.

24. **Irwin, T., Tonkinwise, C., & Kossoff, G. (2022). Transition Design: An Educational Framework for Advancing the Study and Design of Sustainable Transitions.** *Cuadernos del Centro de Estudios en Diseño y Comunicación*, 105, 31–65.
    DOI: 10.18682/cdc.vi105.4188
    - Educational framework for teaching transition design at CMU and beyond.

25. **Tonkinwise, C. (2014). Design Away.** In T. Fry (Ed.), *Design and the Question of History*, Bloomsbury, 198–213.
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474293792.ch-013
    - Argued design must "design away" unsustainable practices; connected to defuturing and transition.

26. **Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Just Design: Being Dogmatic about Defining Speculative Critical Design Future Fiction.** Medium essay / working paper.
    URL: https://medium.com/@camerontw/just-design-b1f97cb3996f
    - Distinguished between speculative design, critical design, design futures, and design fiction as distinct practices.

27. **Tonkinwise, C. (2005). Is Design Finished? Dematerialisation and Changing Things.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 3(2), 99–117.
    DOI: 10.2752/144871305X13966254124680
    - Early work on design and sustainability; questioned whether design should produce less rather than more.

28. **Irwin, T., & Kossoff, G. (2025). Transition Design: Elements of Practice.** *The Design Journal*.
    DOI: 10.1080/14606925.2025.2597448
    - Recent empirical review of 35 transition design cases ten years post-inception; elements of practice.

29. **Kossoff, G. (2019). Cosmopolitan Localism and the Symbiotic City: Transition Design, Nested Economies, and Everyday Life.** *Cuadernos del Centro de Estudios en Diseño y Comunicación*, 73, 55–78.
    DOI: 10.18682/cdc.vi73.1038
    - Developed the nested systems framework for transition design connecting scales from household to region.

## 4. Experiential Futures (Candy, Dunagan)

30. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
    URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
    - Foundational work on experiential futures; designing immersive scenarios to make futures tangible.

31. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
    - Detailed case study of experiential futures methodology at Arizona State University.

32. **Candy, S., & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3–22.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
    - Combined experiential futures with ethnographic methods for community-engaged futuring.

33. **Candy, S., & Potter, C. (Eds.) (2019). Design and Futures.** Tamkang University Press / independently published.
    URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/338129083_Design_and_Futures
    - Collected volume on the intersection of design and futures studies; 30 essays from 49 contributors across 16 countries.

34. **Candy, S., & Watson, J. (2018). Gaming Futures Literacy: The Thing From The Future.** In R. Miller (Ed.), *Transforming the Future*, Routledge/UNESCO.
    DOI: 10.4324/9781351048002-7
    - Presented "The Thing From The Future" card game as a method for popularising futures literacy through play.

35. **Dunagan, J. (2019). Strategic Foresight Studio: A First-Hand Account of an Experiential Futures Laboratory.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3).
    URL: https://jfsdigital.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/05-Dunagan-Strategic-Foresight-Studio.pdf
    - First-hand account of designing and running an experiential futures lab.

36. **Candy, S. (2018). Experiential Futures: A Brief Outline.** Blog post / working paper.
    URL: https://futuryst.blogspot.com/2018/10/experiential-futures-brief-outline.html
    - Concise overview of the experiential futures framework and its design applications.

## 5. Speculative & Critical Design (Dunne & Raby, Auger)

37. **Dunne, A. (1999/2005). Hertzian Tales: Electronic Products, Aesthetic Experience, and Critical Design.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-54199-2
    - Introduced "critical design"; positioned electronic products as vehicles for critique and reflection.

38. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2001). Design Noir: The Secret Life of Electronic Objects.** Birkhäuser / Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-3-7643-6566-0 | DOI: 10.5040/9781350070660
    - Extended critical design into narrative design practice; electronic objects as carriers of hidden stories.

39. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
    URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/speculative-everything/
    - Landmark text on speculative design; introduced the PPPP futures cone widely adopted in design futuring.

40. **Auger, J. (2013). Speculative Design: Crafting the Speculation.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 11–35.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.767276
    - Examined how speculative design constructs its speculations; "alternative presents" as a design strategy.

41. **Auger, J. (2010). Alternative Presents and Speculative Futures: Designing Fictions Through the Extrapolation and Evasion of Product Lineages.** *Negotiating Futures – Design Fiction*, Swiss Design Network, 6, 42–57.
    URL: https://researchonline.rca.ac.uk/1093/
    - Proposed "alternative presents" — stepping out of a product lineage at a past point to reimagine the present.

42. **Bardzell, J., & Bardzell, S. (2013). What Is "Critical" About Critical Design?** *Proceedings of CHI '13*, 3297–3306.
    DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451
    - HCI critique of critical/speculative design; analysed its claims and methods from an interaction design perspective.

43. **Wong, R. Y., & Khovanskaya, V. (2018). Speculative Design in HCI: From Corporate Imaginations to Critical Orientations.** In *New Directions in Third Wave HCI*, Springer, 175–202.
    DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73374-6_10
    - Mapped the landscape of speculative design in HCI; distinguished corporate from critical orientations.

44. **Wong, R. Y., Khovanskaya, V., Fox, S. E., Merrill, N., & Sengers, P. (2020). Infrastructural Speculations: Tactics for Designing and Interrogating Lifeworlds.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376515
    - Introduced "infrastructural speculations" — speculative design foregrounding the lifeworld of artifacts within broader systems.

## 6. Design Fiction (Sterling, Bleecker, Lindley & Coulton)

45. **Sterling, B. (2005). Shaping Things.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-69326-7
    - Coined the term "design fiction"; introduced "spimes" as designed objects aware of their own history.

46. **Bleecker, J. (2009). Design Fiction: A Short Essay on Design, Science, Fact and Fiction.** Near Future Laboratory.
    URL: https://blog.nearfuturelaboratory.com/2009/03/17/design-fiction-a-short-essay-on-design-science-fact-and-fiction/
    - Foundational essay establishing design fiction by combining Sterling's concept with Kirby's diegetic prototypes.

47. **Kirby, D. A. (2010). The Future is Now: Diegetic Prototypes and the Role of Popular Films in Generating Real-World Technological Development.** *Social Studies of Science*, 40(1), 41–70.
    DOI: 10.1177/0306312709338325
    - Coined "diegetic prototypes" — technologies that exist as fully functioning objects within fictional worlds; key concept for design fiction.

48. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2015). Back to the Future: 10 Years of Design Fiction.** *Proceedings of the 2015 British HCI Conference*, ACM, 210–211.
    DOI: 10.1145/2783446.2783592
    - Reviewed a decade of design fiction practice and discourse; assessed maturity of the field.

49. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2016). Pushing the Limits of Design Fiction: The Case For Fictional Research Papers.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 4032–4043.
    DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858446
    - Proposed fictional research papers as a form of design fiction; expanded the genre beyond artifacts.

50. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J., Sturdee, M., & Stead, M. (2017). Design Fiction as World Building.** *Proceedings of Research Through Design Conference 2017*.
    DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.4746964
    - Argued design fiction is fundamentally a "world building" activity, not inherently tied to narrative or storytelling.

51. **Lindley, J., Coulton, P., & Akmal, H. A. (2016). Design Fiction: Does the Search for Plausibility Lead to Deception?** *Proceedings of DRS '16*.
    URL: https://dl.designresearchsociety.org/drs-conference-papers/drs2016/researchpapers/49/
    - Examined whether crafting plausibility in design fictions can become deceptive; ethics of design fiction.

52. **Blythe, M. (2014). Research Through Design Fiction: Narrative in Real and Imaginary Abstracts.** *Proceedings of CHI '14*, 703–712.
    DOI: 10.1145/2556288.2557098
    - Proposed fictional abstracts as design fiction research method; explored narrative as design material.

53. **Tanenbaum, T. J. (2014). Design Fictional Interactions: Why HCI Should Care About Stories.** *Interactions*, 21(5), 22–23.
    DOI: 10.1145/2648414
    - Argued HCI should attend to narrative and fiction as core design materials for envisioning futures.

54. **Gonzatto, R. F., van Amstel, F. M. C., Merkle, L. E., & Hartmann, T. (2013). The Ideology of the Future in Design Fictions.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 36–45.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.772524
    - Critical analysis of ideological assumptions embedded in design fiction and futuring practices.

55. **Grand, S., & Wiedmer, M. (2010). Design Fiction: A Method Toolbox for Design Research in a Complex World.** *Proceedings of DRS '10*.
    URL: https://dl.designresearchsociety.org/drs-conference-papers/drs2010/researchpapers/47/
    - Proposed design fiction as a structured method toolbox for complex design research challenges.

56. **Markussen, T., & Knutz, E. (2013). The Poetics of Design Fiction.** *Proceedings of DPPI '13*, 231–240.
    DOI: 10.1145/2513506.2513531
    - Developed a poetics framework for design fiction drawing on literary theory.

## 7. Participatory Futures (Mazé, Light, Akama)

57. **Mazé, R. (2019). Politics of Designing Visions of the Future.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 23–38.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0003
    - Analysed the political dimensions of design-led futuring; who gets to envision whose future.

58. **Mazé, R., & Redström, J. (2009). Difficult Forms: Critical Practices of Design and Research.** *Research Design Journal*, 1, 28–39.
    URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/236669842_Difficult_Forms_Critical_practices_of_design_and_research
    - Articulated critical practice in design research; "criticism from within" as methodology for engaging futures.

59. **Light, A., & Akama, Y. (2012). The Human Touch: Participatory Practice and the Role of Facilitation in Designing with Communities.** *Proceedings of PDC '12*, 61–70.
    DOI: 10.1145/2347635.2347645
    - Addressed facilitation in community-oriented futuring; relevant to participatory futures.

60. **Light, A., & Akama, Y. (2014). Structuring Future Social Relations: The Politics of Care in Participatory Practice.** *Proceedings of PDC '14*, 151–160.
    DOI: 10.1145/2661435.2661438
    - Developed a politics-of-care framework for participatory futures; how futures are structured through social relations.

61. **Akama, Y., Light, A., & Kamihira, T. (2020). Expanding Participation to Design with More-Than-Human Concerns.** *Proceedings of PDC '20*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3385010.3385016
    - Extended participatory futures beyond human-centered design to more-than-human concerns.

62. **Jungk, R., & Müllert, N. (1987). Future Workshops: How to Create Desirable Futures.** Institute for Social Inventions.
    ISBN: 978-0-948826-03-1
    - Introduced the Future Workshop method; foundational participatory approach to creating desirable futures.

63. **Bendor, R., Lyons, S. H., & Robinson, J. (2012). What's There Not to "Like"? The Technical Affordances of Sustainability Deliberations on Facebook.** *JeDEM*, 4(1), 67–88.
    DOI: 10.29379/jedem.v4i1.117
    - Explored digital platforms for futures deliberation around sustainability.

64. **Bendor, R. (2018). Interactive Media for Sustainability.** Palgrave Macmillan.
    ISBN: 978-3-319-70382-4 | DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-70383-1
    - Critical account of interactive technologies designed to engage the public with sustainability futures.

65. **Wangel, J., Mazé, R., et al. (2012). Backcasting and Design for Sustainable Social Practices.** Working paper.
    URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/236670395_Backcasting_and_Design_for_Sustainable_Social_Practices
    - Integrated futures studies backcasting with design epistemology and social practice theory.

## 8. Decolonial Futures (Escobar, Ansari, Schultz et al.)

66. **Escobar, A. (2018). Designs for the Pluriverse: Radical Interdependence, Autonomy, and the Making of Worlds.** Duke University Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-8223-7105-2 | DOI: 10.1215/9780822371816
    - Proposed "autonomous design" and "design for the pluriverse"; decolonial approach to design futures — designing "a world where many worlds fit."

67. **Schultz, T., Abdulla, D., Ansari, A., Canlı, E., Keshavarz, M., Kiem, M., Prado de O. Martins, L., & Vieira de Oliveira, P. J. S. (2018). What Is at Stake with Decolonizing Design? A Roundtable.** *Design and Culture*, 10(1), 81–101.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2018.1434368
    - Key text on decolonizing design and futures; critiqued Western-centric futuring by the Decolonising Design Group.

68. **Ansari, A. (2019). A Manifesto for Decolonising Design.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 129–132.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0012
    - Design decolonisation manifesto; appeared in the Candy & Potter special issue on Design and Futures.

69. **Ansari, A. (2019). Decolonizing Design Through the Perspectives of Cosmological Others.** *XRDS: Crossroads, The ACM Magazine for Students*, 26(2), 16–19.
    DOI: 10.1145/3368048
    - Argued for decolonizing design through non-Western cosmological perspectives.

70. **Schultz, T. (2018). Mapping Indigenous Futures: Decolonising Techno-Colonising Designs.** *Strategic Design Research Journal*, 11(2), 79–91.
    DOI: 10.4013/sdrj.2018.112.04
    - Mapped indigenous futures against techno-colonial design; Aboriginal design futures perspective.

71. **Keshavarz, M. (2019). The Design Politics of the Passport: Materiality, Immobility, and Dissent.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-4742-8939-1 | DOI: 10.5040/9781350143081
    - Examined design and politics as inseparable ("design-politics"); passport design as futural governance technology.

72. **Keshavarz, M., & Mazé, R. (2013). Design and Dissensus: Framing and Staging Participation in Design Research.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 11(1), 7–29.
    DOI: 10.2752/089279313X13968799815994
    - Theorised dissensus and participation in design; connected political design to future-making.

73. **Tlostanova, M. (2017). On Decolonizing Design.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 15(1), 51–61.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2017.1301017
    - Extended decolonial critique to design theory; post-Soviet and Global South perspectives on design futures.

77. **DiSalvo, C. (2012). Adversarial Design.** MIT Press.

## Summary

Futuring methods in design trace from futures studies (Dator, Bell, Slaughter, Voros, Inayatullah) through design futuring/defuturing (Fry, Willis), transition design (Irwin, Tonkinwise, Kossoff), experiential futures (Candy, Dunagan), speculative/critical design (Dunne & Raby, Auger), design fiction (Sterling, Bleecker, Lindley & Coulton), participatory futures (Mazé, Light, Akama), decolonial futures (Escobar, Ansari, Schultz, Keshavarz), design for policy (Kimbell, DiSalvo), and design for sustainability transitions (Gaziulusoy, Ceschin, Manzini). They position design as fundamentally about shaping futures — not just products — and engage with systems thinking, sustainability transitions, and questions of whose futures count.

**2025–2026 update:** The field has expanded significantly with meta-reviews (Celik & Kaya 2025; Sanchez et al. 2025), new methods (co-experiential futuring, protocol futuring), Black futuring perspectives (Harrington et al. 2022), more-than-human futures (Wakkary 2021; Tironi et al. 2023), institutional reflections (RCA's decade of futuring), and transition design empirical reviews (Irwin & Kossoff 2025). Kozubaev et al. (2020)'s five reflective modes remain the most cited framework for evaluating design futuring work, while the SPIN framework (Sanchez et al. 2025) provides a new classification for HCI futures research.
