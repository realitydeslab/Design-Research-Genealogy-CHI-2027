# Literature Review: Futuring Methods in Design

## Overview

Futuring methods in design draw from futures studies, foresight, and transition research. Key lineages include transition design (Irwin, Kossoff, Tonkinwise at CMU), design futures (Candy, Dunagan), anticipatory design, and foresight-informed design practice. These approaches position design as a vehicle for imagining and navigating long-term systemic change.

---

## Futures Studies Foundations

1. **Dator, J. (2009). Alternative Futures at the Manoa School.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 14(2), 1–18.
   URL: https://jfsdigital.org/articles-and-essays/2009-2/vol-14-no-2-november/articles/alternative-futures-at-the-manoa-school/
   - Overview of the Manoa School of futures studies; Dator's four generic futures images (continuation, collapse, discipline, transformation).

2. **Slaughter, R. A. (1995). The Foresight Principle: Cultural Recovery in the 21st Century.** Praeger.
   ISBN: 978-0-275-95259-0
   - Foundational foresight text; argued foresight is a necessary capacity for navigating complexity.

3. **Bell, W. (1997). Foundations of Futures Studies: Human Science for a New Era, Volume 1.** Transaction Publishers.
   ISBN: 978-1-56000-271-4
   - Comprehensive philosophical and methodological foundations for futures studies.

4. **Inayatullah, S. (1998). Causal Layered Analysis: Poststructuralism as Method.** *Futures*, 30(8), 815–829.
   DOI: 10.1016/S0016-3287(98)00086-X
   - Introduced Causal Layered Analysis (CLA) — a layered futures methodology moving from litany to metaphor/myth.

5. **Voros, J. (2003). A Generic Foresight Process Framework.** *Foresight*, 5(3), 10–21.
   DOI: 10.1108/14636680310698379
   - Widely used framework for foresight processes; extended Dator's work with the futures cone.

## Transition Design

6. **Irwin, T. (2015). Transition Design: A Proposal for a New Area of Design Practice, Study, and Research.** *Design and Culture*, 7(2), 229–246.
   DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829
   - Foundational paper proposing transition design as a new design discipline focused on societal transitions toward sustainability.

7. **Irwin, T., Kossoff, G., & Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Transition Design Provocation.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 3–11.
   DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085688
   - Laid out the provocative vision for transition design as a field.

8. **Irwin, T. (2018). The Emerging Transition Design Approach.** *Proceedings of DRS '18*, 968–980.
   DOI: 10.21606/drs.2018.210
   - Updated framing of transition design incorporating systems thinking, stakeholder relations, and new ways of designing.

9. **Irwin, T., Tonkinwise, C., & Kossoff, G. (2022). Transition Design: An Educational Framework for Advancing the Study and Design of Sustainable Transitions.** *Cuadernos del Centro de Estudios en Diseño y Comunicación*, 105, 31–65.
   DOI: 10.18682/cdc.vi105.4188
   - Educational framework for teaching transition design.

10. **Kossoff, G. (2015). Holism and the Reconstitution of Everyday Life: A Framework for Transition to a Sustainable Society.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 13(1), 25–38.
    DOI: 10.1080/14487136.2015.1085690
    - Theoretical grounding for transition design in holistic thinking and everyday life.

## Design Futures and Experiential Futures

11. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
    URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
    - Foundational work on experiential futures; designing immersive scenarios to make futures tangible.

12. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
    - Detailed case study of experiential futures methodology.

13. **Candy, S., & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3–22.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
    - Combined experiential futures with ethnographic methods.

14. **Candy, S., & Potter, C. (Eds.) (2019). Design and Futures.** Tamkang University Press.
    URL: https://www.tamkang.edu.tw/
    - Collected volume on the intersection of design and futures studies.

15. **Gonzatto, R. F., van Amstel, F. M. C., Merkle, L. E., & Hartmann, T. (2013). The Ideology of the Future in Design Fictions.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 36–45.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.772524
    - Critical analysis of ideological assumptions embedded in design fiction and futuring practices.

## Anticipatory Design

16. **Mazé, R. (2019). Politics of Designing Visions of the Future.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 23–38.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0003
    - Analyzed the political dimensions of design-led futuring; who gets to envision whose future.

17. **Jungk, R., & Müllert, N. (1987). Future Workshops: How to Create Desirable Futures.** Institute for Social Inventions.
    ISBN: 978-0-948826-03-1
    - Introduced the Future Workshop method; participatory approach to creating desirable futures.

18. **Adam, B., & Groves, C. (2007). Future Matters: Action, Knowledge, Ethics.** Brill.
    ISBN: 978-90-04-16177-0
    DOI: 10.1163/ej.9789004161771.i-218
    - Sociological analysis of how futures are constructed; foundational for design's engagement with temporality.

19. **Appadurai, A. (2013). The Future as Cultural Fact: Essays on the Global Condition.** Verso Books.
    ISBN: 978-1-84467-985-5
    - Argued that the capacity to aspire is unequally distributed; relevant to justice in design futuring.

20. **Schultz, T., Abdulla, D., Ansari, A., Canlı, E., Keshavarz, M., Kiem, M., Prado de O. Martins, L., & Vieira de Oliveira, P. J. S. (2018). What Is at Stake with Decolonizing Design? A Roundtable.** *Design and Culture*, 10(1), 81–101.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2018.1434368
    - Key text on decolonizing design and futures; critiqued Western-centric futuring.

## Strategic Design and Systems Thinking

21. **Manzini, E. (2015). Design, When Everybody Designs: An Introduction to Design for Social Innovation.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-02860-0
    DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/9873.001.0001
    - Proposed design for social innovation; positioned designers as facilitators of distributed design processes.

22. **Manzini, E., & Coad, R. (2019). Politics of the Everyday.** Bloomsbury.
    ISBN: 978-1-350-08753-2
    DOI: 10.5040/9781350087569
    - Extended design for social innovation to everyday politics; design as democratic practice.

23. **Escobar, A. (2018). Designs for the Pluriverse: Radical Interdependence, Autonomy, and the Making of Worlds.** Duke University Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-8223-7105-2
    DOI: 10.1215/9780822371816
    - Proposed "autonomous design" and "design for the pluriverse"; decolonial approach to design futures.

24. **Willis, A.-M. (2006). Ontological Designing.** *Design Philosophy Papers*, 4(2), 69–92.
    DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514
    - Proposed that design designs us back ("ontological designing"); foundational for understanding design's futural dimension.

25. **Tonkinwise, C. (2014). Design Away.** In T. Fry (Ed.), *Design and the Question of History*, Bloomsbury, 198–213.
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474293792.ch-013
    - Argued design must "design away" unsustainable practices; connected to defuturing and transition.

26. **Fry, T. (2009). Design Futuring: Sustainability, Ethics and New Practice.** Berg Publishers.
    ISBN: 978-1-84788-244-3
    DOI: 10.5040/9781474293488
    - Introduced "design futuring" and "defuturing"; argued design is central to whether futures are sustained or destroyed.

## Scenarios and Methods

27. **Schwartz, P. (1991). The Art of the Long View: Planning for the Future in an Uncertain World.** Doubleday.
    ISBN: 978-0-385-26732-5
    - Classic on scenario planning; widely used in design-led futuring.

28. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
    URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/speculative-everything/
    - (Cross-listed with Speculative Design.) The PPPP cone of futures is widely adopted in design futuring.

29. **Ilstedt, S., & Wangel, J. (2014). Altering Expectations: How Design Fictions and Backcasting Can Leverage Sustainable Lifestyles.** *Proceedings of DRS '14*.
    DOI: 10.21606/drs.2014.17
    - Combined backcasting with design fiction for sustainable futures.

30. **Light, A., & Akama, Y. (2012). The Human Touch: Participatory Practice and the Role of Facilitation in Designing with Communities.** *Proceedings of PDC '12*, 61–70.
    DOI: 10.1145/2347635.2347645
    - Addressed facilitation in community-oriented futuring; relevant to participatory futures.

31. **Bendor, R., Lyons, S. H., & Robinson, J. (2012). What's There Not to "Like"? The Technical Affordances of Sustainability Deliberations on Facebook.** *JeDEM*, 4(1), 67–88.
    DOI: 10.29379/jedem.v4i1.117
    - Explored digital platforms for futures deliberation.

32. **Gaziulusoy, A. İ., & Erdoğan Öztekin, E. (2019). Design for Sustainability Transitions: Origins, Attitudes and Future Directions.** *Sustainability*, 11(13), 3601.
    DOI: 10.3390/su11133601
    - Systematic mapping of design for sustainability transitions literature.

---

## Summary

Futuring methods in design trace from futures studies (Dator, Bell, Slaughter) through transition design (Irwin, 2015), experiential futures (Candy), and decolonial futures (Escobar, Schultz et al.). They position design as fundamentally about shaping futures — not just products — and engage with systems thinking, sustainability transitions, and questions of whose futures count.
