## Foundational Works

1. **Lindley, J., Sharma, D., & Potts, R. (2014). Anticipatory Ethnography: Design Fiction as an Input to Design Ethnography.** *Proceedings of Ethnographic Praxis in Industry Conference (EPIC)*, 237–253.
   DOI: 10.1111/1559-8918.01030
   - The originating paper. Proposed that design fiction artifacts can serve as inputs to ethnographic investigation, enabling the study of futures that don't yet exist. Argued design ethnography can be "unbound from ties to the present."

2. **Lindley, J., Sharma, D., & Potts, R. (2015). Operationalizing Design Fiction with Anticipatory Ethnography.** *Proceedings of Ethnographic Praxis in Industry Conference (EPIC)*, 58–71.
   DOI: 10.1111/1559-8918.2015.01040
   - Follow-up paper that operationalized the method, providing practical guidance for conducting anticipatory ethnographic studies in industry contexts.

3. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2015). Back to the Future: 10 Years of Design Fiction.** *Proceedings of British HCI '15*, 210–211.
   DOI: 10.1145/2783446.2783592
   - Retrospective that positioned anticipatory ethnography within the broader evolution of design fiction as a practice.

## Parent Lineage: Design Fiction

4. **Bleecker, J. (2009). Design Fiction: A Short Essay on Design, Science, Fact and Fiction.** Near Future Laboratory.
   URL: https://blog.nearfuturelaboratory.com/2009/03/17/design-fiction-a-short-essay-on-design-science-fact-and-fiction/
   - Formalized design fiction; "diegetic prototypes" are the artifacts that anticipatory ethnography then studies.

5. **Sterling, B. (2005). Shaping Things.** MIT Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-262-69326-7
   - Coined "design fiction" as a practice; the conceptual precursor.

6. **Kirby, D. (2010). The Future is Now: Diegetic Prototypes and the Role of Popular Films in Generating Real-World Technological Development.** *Social Studies of Science*, 40(1), 41–70.
   DOI: 10.1177/0306312709338325
   - Introduced "diegetic prototypes" — the theoretical backbone for understanding what anticipatory ethnography analyzes.

7. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2016). Pushing the Limits of Design Fiction: The Case for Fictional Research Papers.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 4032–4043.
   DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858446
   - Extended design fiction forms; fictional papers as artifacts for anticipatory ethnographic analysis.

8. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J., Sturdee, M., & Stead, M. (2017). Design Fiction as World Building.** *Proceedings of the 3rd Biennial Research Through Design Conference*.
   URL: https://figshare.com/articles/journal_contribution/Design_Fiction_as_World_Building/4746964
   - Reframed design fiction as world building rather than individual artifact creation; anticipatory ethnography can study entire fictional worlds.

## Parent Lineage: Ethnography in Design

9. **Crabtree, A., Rouncefield, M., & Tolmie, P. (2012). Doing Design Ethnography.** Springer.
   DOI: 10.1007/978-1-4471-2726-0
   - Comprehensive guide to design ethnography — the practice that anticipatory ethnography extends temporally.

10. **Dourish, P. (2006). Implications for Design.** *Proceedings of CHI '06*, 541–550.
    DOI: 10.1145/1124772.1124855
    - Critiqued the "implications for design" model; argued ethnography's value exceeds design recommendations. Anticipatory ethnography responds by offering a different model of ethnographic contribution.

11. **Suchman, L. (2007). Human-Machine Reconfigurations: Plans and Situated Actions** (2nd ed.). Cambridge University Press.
    DOI: 10.1017/CBO9780511808418
    - Foundational for design ethnography; anticipatory ethnography extends Suchman's situated analysis to fictional situations.

## Related Hybrid Methods

12. **Elsden, C., Chatting, D., Durrant, A. C., Garbett, A., Nissen, B., Vines, J., & Kirk, D. S. (2017). On Speculative Enactments.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 5386–5399.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503
    - "Speculative enactments" — a parallel hybrid method combining design fiction with user study. Shares anticipatory ethnography's impulse to study futures through designed artifacts.

13. **Candy, S., & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3–22.
    DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
    - Combined experiential futures with ethnographic methods. Parallel development to anticipatory ethnography from the futures studies side.

14. **Blythe, M. (2014). Research through Design Fiction: Narrative in Real and Imaginary Abstracts.** *Proceedings of CHI '14*, 703–712.
    DOI: 10.1145/2556288.2557098
    - Used design fiction as a research method; early example of studying fictional artifacts analytically.

15. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J., & Cooper, R. (2018). The Little Book of Design Fiction for the Internet of Things.** Lancaster University.
    URL: https://www.petrashub.org/the-little-book-of-design-fiction-for-the-internet-of-things/
    - Practical guide applying design fiction (and implicitly anticipatory ethnography) to IoT contexts.

## Design + Ethnography + Futures (Akama & Pink)

16. **Akama, Y., Pink, S., & Fergusson, A. (2015). Design + Ethnography + Futures: Surrendering in Uncertainty.** *Proceedings of the 33rd Annual ACM Conference Extended Abstracts on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI EA '15)*, 531–542.
    URL: https://dblp.org/pid/26/8855.html
    - Proposed "surrendering in uncertainty" as a stance for combining design, ethnography, and futures. Parallel to anticipatory ethnography from a design anthropology tradition.

17. **Akama, Y., Pink, S., & Sumartojo, S. (2018). Uncertainty and Possibility: New Approaches to Future Making in Design Anthropology.** Bloomsbury Academic.
    ISBN: 978-1-350-00270-8
    - Book-length treatment of merging design anthropology with futures. Positioned speculative/anticipatory ethnographic methods within broader design anthropology.

18. **Pink, S. (2014). Digital–Visual–Sensory-Design Anthropology: Ethnography, Imagination and Intervention.** *Arts and Humanities in Higher Education*, 13(4), 412–427.
    DOI: 10.1177/1474022214542353
    - Connected sensory/visual ethnography to design anthropology; laid groundwork for anticipatory approaches by emphasizing ethnographic imagination.

## Methodological Context

19. **Hammersley, M., & Atkinson, P. (2007). Ethnography: Principles in Practice** (3rd ed.). Routledge.
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203944769
    - Standard ethnographic methods reference; anticipatory ethnography departs from these by studying fictional rather than actual settings.

20. **Marcus, G. E. (1995). Ethnography in/of the World System: The Emergence of Multi-Sited Ethnography.** *Annual Review of Anthropology*, 24, 95–117.
    DOI: 10.1146/annurev.an.24.100195.000523
    - Multi-sited ethnography as precedent for extending ethnographic scope beyond a single present site — anticipatory ethnography extends this temporally.

21. **Lindley, J., Coulton, P., & Sturdee, M. (2017). Implications for Adoption.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 265–277.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025742
    - Satirical design fiction paper; demonstrates the kind of artifact that anticipatory ethnography can analyze.

22. **Tanenbaum, T. J. (2014). Design Fictional Interactions: Why HCI Should Care about Stories.** *Interactions*, 21(5), 22–23.
    DOI: 10.1145/2648414
    - Argued for narrative as first-class concern in HCI; relevant to anticipatory ethnography's use of fictional narratives.

## More-than-Human Extensions

23. **Coulton, P., & Lindley, J. (2019). More-Than Human Centred Design: Considering Other Things.** *The Design Journal*, 22(4), 463–481.
    DOI: 10.1080/14606925.2019.1614320
    - Applied Object-Oriented Ontology to design; connected Lindley's design fiction work to more-than-human perspectives, extending anticipatory ethnography's scope beyond human futures.

24. **Nicenboim, I., Lindley, J., & Redström, J. (2024). More-than-human Design and AI: Exploring the Space between Theory and Practice.** *Proceedings of DRS 2024*, Boston.
    URL: https://dl.designresearchsociety.org/drs-conference-papers/drs2024/researchpapers/297/
    - Recent work bridging more-than-human design with AI; extends anticipatory ethnography's methods to nonhuman agents and AI systems.

25. **Akama, Y., Keen, S., & West, P. (2016). Speculative Design and Heterogeneity in Indigenous Nation Building.** *Proceedings of DIS '16*, 895–899.
    DOI: 10.1145/2901790.2901852
    - Applied speculative/anticipatory methods in Indigenous contexts; demonstrates how anticipatory ethnographic approaches can be culturally responsive.

## Recent Developments (2020–2026)

26. **Nicenboim, I., Søndergaard, M. L. J., Lindley, J., Reddy, A., Strengers, Y., Redström, J., & Giaccardi, E. (2024). Unmaking-with AI: Tactics for Decentering through Design.** *ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction*, 31(6), Article 82.
    DOI: 10.1145/3685275
    - Proposes decentering tactics for designing with AI; connects anticipatory ethnography to emerging AI design challenges through more-than-human lenses.

    - Bridges posthuman theory with design practice; includes trio-ethnography as method; extends anticipatory ethnography into posthuman territory.

28. **Akama, Y., Stuedahl, D., & van Zyl, I. (2015). Design Disruption in Contested, Contingent and Contradictory Future-Making.** *Interaction Design and Architecture(s) (IxD&A)*, 26, 132–148.
    URL: https://dblp.org/pid/26/8855.html
    - Explored how design can disrupt dominant future narratives; parallel to anticipatory ethnography's critical capacity.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **Design Fiction** (parent): Anticipatory Ethnography studies design fiction artifacts ethnographically
- **Ethnography** (parent): Extends ethnographic methods to fictional/future contexts
- **More-than-Human Design**: Lindley's work bridges anticipatory ethnography to more-than-human perspectives via OOO
- **Thing Ethnography**: Shares the impulse to study from non-standard perspectives (futures vs. things)
- **Foresight**: Both aim to understand futures, but through different methods (analytical vs. ethnographic)
- **World Building**: The fictional worlds created through world building become sites for anticipatory ethnographic analysis
- **Speculative Enactments**: Parallel hybrid method; Elsden et al. combine design fiction with situated user studies

## Summary

Anticipatory Ethnography was coined by Lindley, Sharma & Potts (2014) as a hybrid of Design Fiction and Ethnography. It extends ethnographic temporal scope into the future by treating design fiction artifacts as ethnographic sites. The method addresses a gap between design fiction's speculative ambition and ethnography's analytical rigor, enabling researchers to study possible futures with the same depth applied to present contexts.

A parallel trajectory emerged from Akama, Pink, and Sumartojo's Design+Ethnography+Futures program at RMIT (2015–2018), which merged design anthropology with futures thinking through a stance of "surrendering in uncertainty." While anticipatory ethnography begins with design fiction artifacts, the DEF approach begins with participants' lived experiences and speculates forward from there.

More recently (2020–2026), the method has evolved through its connection to more-than-human design. Lindley's collaboration with Nicenboim, Redström, and Giaccardi has extended anticipatory ethnographic thinking to nonhuman agents and AI systems, asking not just what futures humans might inhabit, but what futures emerge from human-nonhuman entanglements. The DRS 2024 papers on "More-than-human Design in Practice" and the TOCHI paper on "Unmaking-with AI" represent the current frontier of this lineage.
