# Literature Review: Participatory Design & Co-Design

## Overview

Participatory Design (PD) originated in Scandinavian workplace democracy movements of the 1970s–80s, emphasizing workers' rights to shape their tools. It evolved into co-design (Sanders & Stappers, 2008), expanding participation beyond workplaces to communities, marginalized groups, and civic contexts. PD is a foundational lineage for design research — many subsequent approaches (RtD, speculative design, more-than-human design) either build on or react against PD's commitments.

---

## Scandinavian Origins

1. **Ehn, P. (1988). Work-Oriented Design of Computer Artifacts.** PhD thesis, Arbetslivscentrum / Almqvist & Wiksell International.
   ISBN: 978-91-7400-933-8
   URL: https://portal.research.lu.se/en/publications/work-oriented-design-of-computer-artifacts
   - Foundational PD text; grounded in Scandinavian labor traditions and Wittgensteinian language games.

2. **Ehn, P. (2008). Participation in Design Things.** *Proceedings of PDC '08*, 92–101.
   DOI: 10.1145/1795234.1795248
   - Reconceived PD through "design things" (assemblies of humans and nonhumans), bridging PD with ANT/STS.

3. **Schuler, D., & Namioka, A. (Eds.) (1993). Participatory Design: Principles and Practices.** Lawrence Erlbaum Associates.
   ISBN: 978-0-8058-0951-2
   DOI: 10.1201/9780203744338
   - Early comprehensive collection on PD principles, methods, and case studies.

4. **Greenbaum, J., & Kyng, M. (Eds.) (1991). Design at Work: Cooperative Design of Computer Systems.** Lawrence Erlbaum Associates.
   ISBN: 978-0-8058-0612-2
   URL: https://dl.acm.org/doi/book/10.5555/125765
   - Seminal collection on cooperative design from the Scandinavian tradition.

5. **Bødker, S. (1991). Through the Interface: A Human Activity Approach to User Interface Design.** Lawrence Erlbaum Associates.
   ISBN: 978-0-8058-0569-9
   - Applied activity theory to participatory system design; foundational for Scandinavian HCI.

6. **Bødker, S., & Kyng, M. (2018). Participatory Design That Matters — Facing the Big Issues.** *ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction*, 25(1), Article 1.
   DOI: 10.1145/3152421
   - Reflected on PD's evolution over 35 years; called for PD to engage with societal grand challenges.

## Co-Design and Co-Creation

7. **Sanders, E. B.-N., & Stappers, P. J. (2008). Co-Creation and the New Landscapes of Design.** *CoDesign*, 4(1), 5–18.
   DOI: 10.1080/15710880701875068
   - Mapped the shift from user-centered design to co-design; distinguished "design-led" from "research-led" approaches.

8. **Sanders, E. B.-N., & Stappers, P. J. (2012). Convivial Toolbox: Generative Research for the Front End of Design.** BIS Publishers.
   ISBN: 978-90-6369-284-1
   - Comprehensive toolkit for generative design research methods (probes, collages, toolkits).

9. **Sanders, E. B.-N. (2002). From User-Centered to Participatory Design Approaches.** In J. Frascara (Ed.), *Design and the Social Sciences*, Taylor & Francis, 1–8.
   DOI: 10.1201/9780203301302.ch1
   - Charted the historical shift from user-centered to participatory approaches.

10. **Simonsen, J., & Robertson, T. (Eds.) (2013). Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design.** Routledge.
    ISBN: 978-0-415-69440-7
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203108543
    - Comprehensive handbook covering PD theory, methods, and domains.

11. **Robertson, T., & Simonsen, J. (2013). Participatory Design: An Introduction.** In *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design*, 1–17.
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203108543-8
    - Overview chapter defining PD's core commitments: mutual learning, shared decision-making, democratic participation.

## Methods and Tools

12. **Gaver, W., Dunne, A., & Pacenti, E. (1999). Design: Cultural Probes.** *Interactions*, 6(1), 21–29.
    DOI: 10.1145/291224.291235
    - Introduced cultural probes as a participatory method; influential across PD, RtD, and speculative design.

13. **Mattelmäki, T. (2006). Design Probes.** PhD thesis, University of Art and Design Helsinki.
    ISBN: 951-558-212-X
    URL: https://aaltodoc.aalto.fi/handle/123456789/11829
    - Extended cultural probes into a systematic design research method.

14. **Brandt, E. (2006). Designing Exploratory Design Games: A Framework for Participation in Participatory Design.** *Proceedings of PDC '06*, 57–66.
    DOI: 10.1145/1900441.1900452
    - Proposed design games as structured participatory activities.

15. **Brandt, E., Binder, T., & Sanders, E. B.-N. (2013). Tools and Techniques: Ways to Engage Telling, Making, and Enacting.** In *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design*, 145–181.
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203108543-14
    - Comprehensive overview of PD tools categorized by telling, making, and enacting.

16. **Vines, J., Clarke, R., Wright, P., McCarthy, J., & Olivier, P. (2013). Configuring Participation: On How We Involve People in Design.** *Proceedings of CHI '13*, 429–438.
    DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2470716
    - Analyzed how participation is configured in design research; questioned assumptions about who participates and how.

## PD with Marginalized Groups

17. **Harrington, C., Erete, S., & Piper, A. M. (2019). Deconstructing Community-Based Collaborative Design: Towards More Equitable Participatory Design Engagements.** *Proceedings of CSCW '19*, Article 98.
    DOI: 10.1145/3359318
    - Critiqued power dynamics in PD with marginalized communities; proposed equity-focused approaches.

18. **Costanza-Chock, S. (2020). Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the Worlds We Need.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-04345-0
    DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001
    - Applied design justice framework; centered marginalized communities' leadership in design.

19. **Le Dantec, C. A., & DiSalvo, C. (2013). Infrastructuring and the Formation of Publics in Participatory Design.** *Social Studies of Science*, 43(2), 241–264.
    DOI: 10.1177/0306312712471581
    - Introduced "infrastructuring" as ongoing PD practice beyond project-bounded participation.

20. **Asad, M., & Le Dantec, C. A. (2015). Illegitimate Civic Participation: Supporting Community Activists on the Ground.** *Proceedings of CSCW '15*, 1694–1703.
    DOI: 10.1145/2675133.2675156
    - Explored PD with communities whose civic participation is deemed "illegitimate" by authorities.

21. **Björgvinsson, E., Ehn, P., & Hillgren, P.-A. (2010). Participatory Design and "Democratizing Innovation."** *Proceedings of PDC '10*, 41–50.
    DOI: 10.1145/1900441.1900448
    - Connected PD to innovation democratization; proposed "design things" as agonistic public spaces.

22. **Light, A., & Akama, Y. (2014). Structuring Future Social Relations: The Politics of Care in Participatory Practice.** *Proceedings of PDC '14*, 151–160.
    DOI: 10.1145/2661435.2661438
    - Applied care ethics to PD; examined power and responsibility in participatory practice.

## PD in Broader Contexts

23. **Muller, M. J. (2003). Participatory Design: The Third Space in HCI.** In J. A. Jacko & A. Sears (Eds.), *The Human-Computer Interaction Handbook*, Lawrence Erlbaum, 1051–1068.
    DOI: 10.1201/9781410615862-71
    - Positioned PD as a "third space" between users and designers in HCI.

24. **Luck, R. (2018). What Is It That Makes Participation in Design Participatory Design?** *Design Studies*, 59, 1–8.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.10.002
    - Philosophical analysis of what constitutes genuine participation in PD.

25. **Suchman, L. (2002). Located Accountabilities in Technology Production.** *Scandinavian Journal of Information Systems*, 14(2), Article 7.
    URL: https://aisel.aisnet.org/sjis/vol14/iss2/7/
    - Connected PD to STS; examined accountability in technology production contexts.

26. **Bannon, L. J., & Ehn, P. (2013). Design: Design Matters in Participatory Design.** In *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design*, 37–63.
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203108543-10
    - Reflected on how "design" itself is understood within PD; traced conceptual evolution.

27. **DiSalvo, C., Clement, A., & Pipek, V. (2013). Participatory Design for, with, and by Communities.** In *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design*, 182–209.
    DOI: 10.4324/9780203108543-15
    - Examined PD's expansion from workplace to community contexts.

28. **Iversen, O. S., Halskov, K., & Leong, T. W. (2012). Values-Led Participatory Design.** *CoDesign*, 8(2–3), 87–103.
    DOI: 10.1080/15710882.2012.672575
    - Proposed values as central organizing principle for PD processes.

29. **Bødker, S., Dindler, C., Iversen, O. S., & Smith, R. C. (2022). Participatory Design.** Morgan & Claypool (Synthesis Lectures on HCI).
    DOI: 10.1007/978-3-031-02235-7
    - Recent synthesis of PD covering history, theory, methods, and contemporary challenges.

30. **Winschiers-Theophilus, H., Bidwell, N. J., & Blake, E. (2012). Altering Participation through Interactions and Reflections in Design.** *CoDesign*, 8(2–3), 163–182.
    DOI: 10.1080/15710882.2012.672580
    - Examined PD in postcolonial contexts; challenges of applying Scandinavian PD in Africa.

31. **Smith, R. C., Bossen, C., & Kanstrup, A. M. (2017). Participatory Design in an Era of Participation.** *CoDesign*, 13(2), 65–69.
    DOI: 10.1080/15710882.2017.1310466
    - Editorial reflecting on PD's position in an era of widespread "participation" rhetoric.

32. **Loi, D. (2007). Reflective Probes, Primitive Probes and Playful Triggers.** *Proceedings of DPPI '07*, 233–245.
    DOI: 10.1145/1314161.1314183
    - Extended probe methodology with reflective and playful variants.

---

## Summary

PD originated in Scandinavian workplace democracy (Ehn, Bødker, 1980s), evolved through co-design (Sanders & Stappers, 2008), and expanded to marginalized communities (Harrington, Costanza-Chock) and more-than-human concerns (Akama & Light, 2020). It provides the participatory ethos that permeates virtually all subsequent design research lineages, and its tensions (who participates? on whose terms?) remain central to the field.
