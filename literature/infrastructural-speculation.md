# Infrastructural Speculation & Values-Oriented Speculative Design: Deep Literature Review

## Overview

This lineage represents a distinct orientation within speculative design that foregrounds **infrastructure, values, ethics, and policy** rather than aesthetics or provocation. Centered on Richmond Y. Wong's work at UC Berkeley → Georgia Tech, it bridges speculative design with STS (Science & Technology Studies), infrastructure studies, and values-sensitive design. Where Dunne & Raby's speculative design focuses on gallery objects and provocation, infrastructural speculation focuses on the lifeworlds, regulatory environments, and systemic entanglements in which technologies exist.

The Design Research Observatory classifies Speculative Design as closely related to Critical Design, Design Fiction, and Material Speculation — but infrastructural speculation adds a fourth dimension: the **systemic and institutional** context of technologies.

## Foundational Works

1. **Wong, R. Y., & Jackson, S. J. (2015). Wireless Visions: Infrastructure, Imagination, and US Spectrum Policy.** *Proceedings of CSCW '15*.
   DOI: 10.1145/2675133.2675229
   - Early work connecting infrastructure studies with imagination/visions. Seeds of infrastructural speculation.

2. **Wong, R. Y., & Mulligan, D. K. (2016). When a Product Is Still Fictional: Anticipating and Speculating Futures through Concept Videos.** *Proceedings of DIS '16*.
   DOI: 10.1145/2901790.2901801
   - Analyzed corporate concept videos as sites of speculation. Honorable Mention. Connected speculation to real-world product futures.

3. **Wong, R. Y., Van Wyk, E., & Pierce, J. (2017). Real-Fictional Entanglements: Using Science Fiction and Design Fiction to Interrogate Sensing Technologies.** *Proceedings of DIS '17*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3064663.3064682
   - Developed design workbook methodology combining science fiction with design fiction for values reflection.

4. **Wong, R. Y., Mulligan, D. K., Van Wyk, E., Pierce, J., & Chuang, J. (2017). Eliciting Values Reflections by Engaging Privacy Futures Using Design Workbooks.** *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 1(CSCW), Article 111.
   DOI: 10.1145/3134746
   - Design workbooks as a method for eliciting values reflection through speculative futures.

5. **Wong, R. Y., & Khovanskaya, V. (2018). Speculative Design in HCI: From Corporate Imaginations to Critical Orientations.** In *New Directions in Third Wave HCI*, Vol. 2, Springer, 175–202.
   DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73356-2_11
   - **Key mapping paper.** Mapped the spectrum of speculative design in HCI from corporate to critical. Essential for our genealogy.

6. **Wong, R. Y., Merrill, N., & Chuang, J. (2018). When BCIs have APIs: Design Fictions of Everyday Brain-Computer Interface Adoption.** *Proceedings of DIS '18*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3196709.3196746
   - Applied design fiction to BCI technology futures.

7. **Wong, R. Y., Khovanskaya, V., Fox, S. E., Merrill, N., & Sengers, P. (2020). Infrastructural Speculations: Tactics for Designing and Interrogating Lifeworlds.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376515
   - **Foundational paper for this lineage.** Introduces "infrastructural speculations" — an orientation toward speculative design that foregrounds lifeworlds (social, perceptual, political environments). Provides design tactics connecting speculative design with infrastructure studies (Star, Bowker). Argues speculative designs should attend to cultural, regulatory, environmental, and repair conditions.

8. **Kozubaev, S., Elsden, C., Howell, N., Søndergaard, M.L.J., Merrill, N., Schulte, B., & Wong, R. Y. (2020). Expanding Modes of Reflection in Design Futuring.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376526
   - Workshop expanding how design futuring engages reflection. Connects Wong's values work with Elsden's speculative enactments.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **→ Speculative & Critical Design:** Infrastructural speculation is a BRANCH of speculative design that adds systemic/institutional awareness. Wong & Khovanskaya (2018) explicitly maps the spectrum.
- **→ Design Fiction:** Wong uses design fiction methods (workbooks, memos, concept videos) but orients them toward values and policy rather than provocation.
- **→ Participatory Design:** Wong's design workbooks and activities are participatory methods for values elicitation.
- **→ Feminist HCI / Design Justice:** Collaborations with Sarah Fox (feminist HCI), connections to Costanza-Chock's design justice.
- **→ Foresight:** Wong's work on policy + design connects speculation to governance and foresight.
- **→ World Building:** "Timelines" activity is explicitly a world-building method.
- **→ RtD:** Wong's practice-based methods (workbooks, memos, activities) are RtD contributions.
- **→ More-than-Human:** Soden, Akama, Light connection via uncertainty work.
- **→ STS / Infrastructure Studies:** Direct bridge to Star, Bowker, Jackson infrastructure tradition.

## Implications for the Genealogy

Infrastructural speculation represents a **branching point** in speculative design (circa 2018-2020) where:
1. Speculative design meets infrastructure studies (STS)
2. The focus shifts from **objects** to **lifeworlds** — the systemic contexts in which technologies exist
3. Values, ethics, and policy become central design concerns rather than afterthoughts
4. Methods become participatory (workbooks, activities) rather than designer-centered (gallery objects)

This should be mapped as a distinct node in our genealogy tree, branching from speculative design around 2018 and connecting to:
- Values Sensitive Design (Friedman)
- Infrastructure Studies (Star, Bowker, Jackson)
- Feminist HCI (Bardzell, Fox)
- Design Futuring (the "expanding modes" CHI '20 workshop)

## Summary

Richmond Y. Wong's body of work constitutes a coherent and influential sub-lineage within speculative design that we can call "infrastructural speculation" or "values-oriented speculative design." Beginning with wireless visions (2015) and concept video analysis (2016), crystallizing with the "Infrastructural Speculations" CHI '20 paper, and extending through design fiction memos, workbooks, and values infrastructure work, this lineage bridges speculative design with STS, ethics, policy, and participatory methods. Wong's frequent collaborators — Khovanskaya, Fox, Merrill, Sengers, Pierce — form a network connecting Cornell, UC Berkeley, CMU, and Georgia Tech. The 2024 DIS workshop "Mapping Futures and Futuring in HCI/Design" (Jenkins et al., with Wong) is directly relevant to our genealogy project.
