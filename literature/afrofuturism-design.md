# Literature Review: Afrofuturism and Design

## Overview

Afrofuturism is a multifaceted cultural and intellectual movement that applies the experiences of the African diaspora to speculation and futures thinking. In design research, it functions as a decolonial form of speculative design, demonstrating empathic engagement with the aesthetics, politics, and experiences of the African diaspora. Coined by Mark Dery (1993), it has been taken up in HCI and design as a lens for more inclusive, culturally grounded futures work.

**Observatory Connection:** The Observatory positions Afrofuturism as "a form of speculative design" linked to Speculative Design, demonstrating that speculation is not culturally neutral.

## Foundational Works

1. **Dery, M. (1994). Black to the Future: Interviews with Samuel R. Delany, Greg Tate, and Tricia Rose.** In M. Dery (Ed.), *Flame Wars: The Discourse of Cyberculture*, Duke University Press, 179–222. (Originally published in *South Atlantic Quarterly*, 92(4), 1993, 735–778.)
   DOI: 10.1215/00382876-92-4-735
   - The originating text. Coined "Afrofuturism" and asked: "Can a community whose past has been deliberately rubbed out, and whose energies have subsequently been consumed by the search for legible traces of its history, imagine possible futures?"

2. **Eshun, K. (2003). Further Considerations of Afrofuturism.** *CR: The New Centennial Review*, 3(2), 287–302.
   DOI: 10.1353/ncr.2003.0021
   - Influential theorization; positioned Afrofuturism as counter-memory and counter-future against chronopolitical erasure.

3. **Womack, Y. L. (2013). Afrofuturism: The World of Black Sci-Fi and Fantasy Culture.** Lawrence Hill Books.
   ISBN: 978-1-61374-796-4
   - Accessible overview defining Afrofuturism as "an intersection of imagination, technology, the future and liberation."

4. **Nelson, A. (2002). Introduction: Future Texts.** *Social Text*, 20(2), 1–15.
   DOI: 10.1215/01642472-20-2_71-1
   - Introduced the *Social Text* special issue on Afrofuturism; positioned it within critical race studies and technology.

## Afrofuturism in Design Research

5. **Harrington, C., Dankwa, K., & Igharo, K. (2022). Radical Futures: Supporting Community-Led Design Engagements through an Afrofuturist Speculative Design Toolkit.** *Proceedings of CHI '22*, Article 523.
   DOI: 10.1145/3491102.3501945
   - Key CHI paper. Developed "Building Utopia" toolkit using Afrofuturist lens for participatory speculative design with Black communities.

6. **Ogbonnaya-Ogburu, I. F., Smith, A. D., To, A., & Toyama, K. (2020). Critical Race Theory for HCI.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*, Article 497.
   DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376392
   - Applied critical race theory to HCI; Afrofuturism as one framework for racially aware design research.

7. **Prado de O. Martins, L., & Oliveira, P. (2014). Questioning the "Critical" in Speculative & Critical Design.** Medium / A Parede.
   URL: https://medium.com/a-parede/questioning-the-critical-in-speculative-critical-design-5a355cac2ca4
   - Critiqued SCD's Eurocentrism and privilege; Afrofuturism (and other non-Western speculative traditions) as response to this gap.

8. **Oliveira, P., & Prado de O. Martins, L. (2015). Futuristic Gizmos, Conservative Ideals: On Anachronistic Design.** *Modes of Criticism*, 1.
   URL: https://modesofcriticism.org/futuristic-gizmos-conservative-ideals/
   - Extended the critique: speculative design reproduces colonial power structures unless deliberately decolonized.

## Decolonial Speculative Practice

9. **Schultz, T., Abdulla, D., Ansari, A., Canlı, E., Keshavarz, M., Kiem, M., Prado de O. Martins, L., & Vieira de Oliveira, P. J. S. (2018). What Is at Stake with Decolonizing Design? A Roundtable.** *Design and Culture*, 10(1), 81–101.
   DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2018.1434368
   - Foundational decolonizing design roundtable; Afrofuturism as one of several decolonial design frameworks.

10. **Escobar, A. (2018). Designs for the Pluriverse: Radical Interdependence, Autonomy, and the Making of Worlds.** Duke University Press.
    DOI: 10.1215/9780822371816
    - "Autonomous design" and the pluriverse; design for multiple worldings including Afrodiasporic futures.

11. **Costanza-Chock, S. (2020). Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the Worlds We Need.** MIT Press.
    DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001
    - Design justice framework centers marginalized communities; Afrofuturism as design justice in practice.

12. **Abdulla, D. (2018). Design Otherwise: Towards a Locally-Grounded Practice of Design.** PhD thesis, Goldsmiths, University of London.
    URL: https://research.gold.ac.uk/id/eprint/23130/
    - Explored design from non-Western perspectives; Afrofuturism as example of "design otherwise."

## Afrofuturism as Cultural Theory

13. **Anderson, R., & Jones, C. E. (Eds.) (2016). Afrofuturism 2.0: The Rise of Astro-Blackness.** Lexington Books.
    ISBN: 978-1-4985-1003-0
    - "Afrofuturism 2.0" extending beyond literary/film origins into design, technology, and activism.

14. **Barber, T. E. (2017). Cyborg Grammar? Reading Wangechi Mutu's Non-je ne regrette rien through Afrofuturism.** *NKA: Journal of Contemporary African Art*, 40, 84–93.
    DOI: 10.1215/10757163-3885858
    - Visual Afrofuturism in contemporary African art; relevant to design's visual speculation practices.

15. **Gilroy, P. (1993). The Black Atlantic: Modernity and Double-Consciousness.** Harvard University Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-674-07606-5
    - The Black Atlantic as theoretical context for Afrofuturism; modernity experienced differently from the African diaspora.

16. **Benjamin, R. (2019). Race After Technology: Abolitionist Tools for the New Jim Code.** Polity Press.
    ISBN: 978-1-5095-2643-7
    - Technology as not neutral; racialized design requires Afrofuturist and abolitionist counter-design.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **Speculative Design** (parent): Afrofuturism is a culturally-specific speculative practice that challenges SCD's Western defaults
- **Design Fiction**: Afrofuturist world building creates culturally grounded fictional futures
- **Participatory Design**: Afrofuturist PD centers Black communities as designers of their own futures
- **Design Justice**: Shared commitment to centering marginalized perspectives
- **Foresight**: Afrofuturism challenges whose futures foresight anticipates

---

## Summary

Afrofuturism (Dery 1993; Eshun 2003; Womack 2013) entered design research through critiques of speculative design's Eurocentrism (Prado & Oliveira 2014; Schultz et al. 2018) and was operationalized through community-led design (Harrington et al. 2022). It demonstrates that speculation is culturally situated and that inclusive futures require diverse speculative traditions beyond Western critical design.
