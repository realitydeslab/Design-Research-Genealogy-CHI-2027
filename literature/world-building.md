# Literature Review: World Building

## Overview

World Building in design research refers to the practice of creating internally-consistent imaginary worlds through creativity and speculation, grounded in reality, history, and research. Distinct from but related to Design Fiction, world building emphasizes the creation of coherent fictional worlds rather than individual diegetic artifacts. Coulton and Lindley (2017) argued that Design Fiction is fundamentally a world-building activity with no inherent link to narrative or storytelling.

**Observatory Connection:** The Observatory defines World Building as creating "internally-consistent imaginary worlds" and links it directly to Design Fiction, with worlds serving as contexts for prototyping designs.

## Foundational Works

1. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J. G., Sturdee, M., & Stead, M. (2017). Design Fiction as World Building.** *Proceedings of Research through Design Conference 2017*, Edinburgh.
   DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.4746964
   - Key paper arguing Design Fiction is a "world building" activity rather than a storytelling one. Multiple artifacts describe "entry points" into a fictional world at different scales.

2. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J. G., & Akmal, H. A. (2016). Design Fiction: Does the Search for Plausibility Lead to Deception?** *Proceedings of DRS '16*.
   DOI: 10.21606/drs.2016.148
   - Examined plausibility in design fiction; argued worlds must be internally consistent to be effective.

3. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2015). Game of Drones.** *Proceedings of CHI '15 Extended Abstracts*, 613–618.
   DOI: 10.1145/2702613.2725444
   - Design fiction using world-building techniques to explore drone futures; demonstrated multi-artifact world construction.

## World Building Theory

4. **Wolf, M. J. P. (2012). Building Imaginary Worlds: The Theory and History of Subcreation.** Routledge.
   DOI: 10.4324/9780203096949
   - Comprehensive theory of world building across media; "subcreation" (Tolkien) as the creation of internally-consistent secondary worlds.

5. **Tolkien, J. R. R. (1947). On Fairy-Stories.** In *Essays Presented to Charles Williams*, Oxford University Press, 38–89.
   URL: https://coolcalvary.files.wordpress.com/2018/10/on-fairy-stories1.pdf
   - The originating theory of "subcreation" — creating secondary worlds with internal consistency. Referenced widely in design fiction world-building literature.

6. **Jenkins, H. (2006). Convergence Culture: Where Old and New Media Collide.** NYU Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-8147-4295-2
   - Transmedia storytelling: building worlds across multiple media platforms. Directly relevant to Design Fiction's multi-artifact world building.

7. **Ryan, M.-L. (2014). Story/Worlds/Media: Tuning the Instruments of a Media-Conscious Narratology.** In M.-L. Ryan & J.-N. Thon (Eds.), *Storyworlds across Media*, University of Nebraska Press, 25–49.
   DOI: 10.2307/j.ctt1d9nkdg.6
   - Narratological theory of story worlds; relevant to understanding how fictional worlds function as design research contexts.

## World Building in Design Practice

8. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
   - Speculative design as world-making; proposed futures exist as imagined worlds populated by designed artifacts.

9. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
   URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
   - Experiential futures as world building: creating immersive scenarios that participants can inhabit. Parallel to design fiction world building from the futures studies side.

10. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
    - Detailed case study of experiential futures world building.

11. **Elsden, C., Chatting, D., Durrant, A. C., Garbett, A., Nissen, B., Vines, J., & Kirk, D. S. (2017). On Speculative Enactments.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 5386–5399.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503
    - Speculative enactments create temporary worlds that participants inhabit and respond to.

12. **Auger, J. (2013). Speculative Design: Crafting the Speculation.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 11–35.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.767276
    - Analyzed how speculative designs construct plausible worlds; craft of world building as design method.

## IoT and Fictional Worlds

13. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J., & Cooper, R. (2018). The Little Book of Design Fiction for the Internet of Things.** Lancaster University.
    URL: https://www.petrashub.org/the-little-book-of-design-fiction-for-the-internet-of-things/
    - Applied design fiction world building to IoT: creating fictional worlds populated by connected devices.

14. **Lindley, J., Coulton, P., & Sturdee, M. (2017). Implications for Adoption.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 265–277.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025742
    - Satirical design fiction demonstrating world building: a fictional world where design fiction is mainstream.

15. **Stead, M. (2017). Designing Game Worlds and Game Writing for Internet of Things Products and Services.** *Proceedings of Research through Design Conference 2017*.
    URL: https://figshare.com/articles/conference_contribution/Designing_Game_Worlds/4747036
    - Connected game world design to IoT product/service design; games as world-building template.

## Critical Perspectives

16. **Gonzatto, R. F., van Amstel, F. M. C., Merkle, L. E., & Hartmann, T. (2013). The Ideology of the Future in Design Fictions.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 36–45.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.772524
    - Critiqued ideological assumptions embedded in fictional worlds; worlds are not neutral but ideologically loaded.

17. **Wong, R. Y., & Khovanskaya, V. (2018). Speculative Design in HCI: From Corporate Imaginations to Critical Orientations.** In *New Directions in Third Wave HCI*, Springer, 175–202.
    DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73356-2_11
    - Mapped whose worlds get built: corporate vs. critical orientations in speculative world building.

## Connections to Other Lineages

- **Design Fiction** (parent): World Building is the core activity of Design Fiction (Coulton & Lindley 2017)
- **Speculative Design**: Fictional worlds as the context for speculative proposals
- **Foresight**: Scenario planning creates worlds (albeit less materially grounded)
- **Experiential Futures** (Candy): Immersive worlds that participants inhabit
- **Anticipatory Ethnography**: Fictional worlds become sites for ethnographic analysis

---

## Summary

World Building in design research was articulated by Coulton, Lindley et al. (2017) as the fundamental activity underlying Design Fiction. Rather than storytelling, Design Fiction creates internally-consistent fictional worlds populated by multiple artifacts at different scales. The approach draws on narratological theory (Tolkien's subcreation, Wolf's imaginary worlds) and connects to experiential futures, scenario planning, and IoT design.
