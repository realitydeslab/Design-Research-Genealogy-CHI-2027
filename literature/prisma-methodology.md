# Literature Review: PRISMA & Systematic Reviews in Design Research

## Overview

Systematic and scoping reviews are increasingly used in design research and HCI to map bodies of knowledge. This section covers the PRISMA guidelines, the PRISMA-ScR extension for scoping reviews, and examples of systematic/scoping reviews applied to design research topics.

---

## PRISMA Guidelines

1. **Page, M. J., McKenzie, J. E., Bossuyt, P. M., Boutron, I., Hoffmann, T. C., Mulrow, C. D., ... & Moher, D. (2021). The PRISMA 2020 Statement: An Updated Guideline for Reporting Systematic Reviews.** *BMJ*, 372, n71.
   DOI: 10.1136/bmj.n71
   - The current PRISMA 2020 guidelines for systematic review reporting; 27-item checklist.

2. **Moher, D., Liberati, A., Tetzlaff, J., Altman, D. G., & The PRISMA Group (2009). Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses: The PRISMA Statement.** *PLoS Medicine*, 6(7), e1000097.
   DOI: 10.1371/journal.pmed.1000097
   - Original PRISMA statement; established the standard for systematic review reporting.

3. **Tricco, A. C., Lillie, E., Zarin, W., O'Brien, K. K., Colquhoun, H., Levac, D., ... & Straus, S. E. (2018). PRISMA Extension for Scoping Reviews (PRISMA-ScR): Checklist and Explanation.** *Annals of Internal Medicine*, 169(7), 467–473.
   DOI: 10.7326/M18-0850
   - PRISMA extension specifically for scoping reviews; the methodological standard for this paper.

4. **Arksey, H., & O'Malley, L. (2005). Scoping Studies: Towards a Methodological Framework.** *International Journal of Social Research Methodology*, 8(1), 19–32.
   DOI: 10.1080/1364557032000119616
   - Foundational framework for scoping studies/reviews; widely adopted in HCI.

5. **Levac, D., Colquhoun, H., & O'Brien, K. K. (2010). Scoping Studies: Advancing the Methodology.** *Implementation Science*, 5, Article 69.
   DOI: 10.1186/1748-5908-5-69
   - Enhanced Arksey & O'Malley's framework with recommendations for each stage.

6. **Peters, M. D. J., Godfrey, C. M., Khalil, H., McInerney, P., Parker, D., & Soares, C. B. (2015). Guidance for Conducting Systematic Scoping Reviews.** *International Journal of Evidence-Based Healthcare*, 13(3), 141–146.
   DOI: 10.1097/XEB.0000000000000050
   - JBI guidance for conducting scoping reviews; complements PRISMA-ScR.

## Systematic Reviews in HCI and Design

7. **Muller, M. J., Lange, I., Wang, D., Piorkowski, D., Tsay, J., Liao, Q. V., Dugan, C., & Erickson, T. (2019). How Data Science Workers Work with Data: Discovery, Capture, Curation, Design, Creation.** *Proceedings of CHI '19*, Article 126.
   DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300356
   - Example of systematic survey methodology in HCI.

8. **Kitchenham, B. (2004). Procedures for Performing Systematic Reviews.** Keele University Technical Report TR/SE-0401.
   URL: https://www.elsevier.com/__data/promis_misc/525444systematicreviewsguide.pdf
   - Widely cited guide for systematic reviews in software engineering; applicable to HCI.

9. **Petticrew, M., & Roberts, H. (2006). Systematic Reviews in the Social Sciences: A Practical Guide.** Blackwell.
   ISBN: 978-1-4051-2110-1
   DOI: 10.1002/9780470754887
   - Comprehensive guide for systematic reviews in social sciences; relevant for design research.

10. **Brereton, P., Kitchenham, B. A., Budgen, D., Turner, M., & Khalil, M. (2007). Lessons from Applying the Systematic Literature Review Process within the Software Engineering Domain.** *Journal of Systems and Software*, 80(4), 571–583.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.jss.2006.07.009
    - Practical lessons for conducting SLRs in computing fields.

## Examples in Design Research

11. **Forlano, L., & Mathew, A. (2014). From Design Fiction to Design Friction: Speculative and Participatory Design of Values-Embedded Urban Technology.** *Journal of Urban Technology*, 21(4), 7–24.
    DOI: 10.1080/10630732.2014.971525
    - Not a systematic review itself but demonstrates the kind of cross-cutting synthesis our genealogy aims to produce.

12. **Gaziulusoy, A. İ., & Erdoğan Öztekin, E. (2019). Design for Sustainability Transitions: Origins, Attitudes and Future Directions.** *Sustainability*, 11(13), 3601.
    DOI: 10.3390/su11133601
    - Systematic mapping of design for sustainability transitions; exemplar methodology for our review.

13. **Lazar, A., Edasis, C., & Piper, A. M. (2017). A Systematic Review of Design Research and HCI Focused on Older Adults.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 155–166.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025619
    - Systematic review in HCI; demonstrates PRISMA-style screening in design contexts.

14. **Maguire, M. (2001). Methods to Support Human-Centred Design.** *International Journal of Human-Computer Studies*, 55(4), 587–634.
    DOI: 10.1006/ijhc.2001.0503
    - Comprehensive review of human-centred design methods; precursor to systematic reviews in the field.

15. **Almeida, T., Comber, R., Wood, G., Saraf, D., & Balaam, M. (2016). On Looking at the Vagina through Labella.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 1810–1821.
    DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858119
    - Example of feminist design research that would appear in a genealogy; demonstrates cross-cutting themes.

16. **Ciolfi Felice, M., Raincap, L., Debaveye, S., Pepping, G., Desmet, P. M. A., & Balaam, M. (2022). Charting the Landscape of HCI Research on Menstruation.** *Proceedings of CHI '22*, Article 577.
    DOI: 10.1145/3491102.3502098
    - Systematic literature review within HCI; good methodological exemplar.

17. **Sanches, P., Janson, A., Karpashevich, P., Nadal, C., Qu, C., Daudén Roquet, C., ... & Höök, K. (2019). HCI and Affective Health: Taking Stock of a Decade of Studies and Charting Future Research Directions.** *Proceedings of CHI '19*, Article 245.
    DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300475
    - Decade-spanning review in HCI using systematic methods.

18. **Mubin, O., Novoa, M., & Al Mahmud, A. (2022). A Review of Research through Design in HCI: A Thematic Analysis.** *International Journal of Human-Computer Interaction*, 38(15), 1479–1490.
    DOI: 10.1080/10447318.2021.2004251
    - Direct precedent: a systematic review of RtD literature in HCI.

19. **Blythe, M., Andersen, K., Clarke, R., & Wright, P. (2016). Anti-Solutionist Strategies: Seriously Silly Design Fiction.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 4968–4978.
    DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858482
    - Demonstrates critical synthesis across design fiction literature.

20. **Chivukula, S. S., Watkins, C. R., Manocha, R., Chen, J., & Gray, C. M. (2020). Dimensions of UX Practice That Shape Ethical Awareness.** *Proceedings of CHI '20*, Article 378.
    DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376459
    - Systematic analysis connecting design practice to ethical dimensions.

---

## Summary

Our paper adopts the PRISMA-ScR framework (Tricco et al., 2018) building on Arksey & O'Malley's (2005) scoping review methodology. The approach is validated by growing precedent for systematic reviews in HCI (Lazar et al., 2017; Sanches et al., 2019; Mubin et al., 2022). The genealogical mapping adds a novel dimension: tracing methodological lineages rather than just thematic clusters.
