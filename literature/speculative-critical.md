# Literature Review: Speculative & Critical Design

## Overview

Speculative and Critical Design (SCD) emerged from the Royal College of Art in the late 1990s–2000s through Anthony Dunne and Fiona Raby's practice. It uses design to provoke debate about possible futures rather than solve present problems. Related lineages include design fiction (Sterling, Bleecker, Lindley & Coulton), adversarial design (DiSalvo), and critical making (Ratto).

## Foundational Works

1. **Dunne, A. (1999). Hertzian Tales: Electronic Products, Aesthetic Experience, and Critical Design.** RCA CRD Research Publications / MIT Press (2005 reissue).
   ISBN: 978-0-262-54178-7
   URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262541787/hertzian-tales/
   - Origin text for critical design. Proposed design as a medium for cultural commentary on electronic products.

2. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2001). Design Noir: The Secret Life of Electronic Objects.** Birkhäuser.
   ISBN: 978-3-7643-6566-2
   - Extended critical design through speculative product proposals exploring the psychological dimensions of technology.

3. **Dunne, A., & Raby, F. (2013). Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming.** MIT Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-262-01984-1
   URL: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/speculative-everything/
   - Definitive statement of speculative design. Introduced the "PPPP" cone of futures (probable, plausible, possible, preferable).

4. **Malpass, M. (2017). Critical Design in Context: History, Theory, and Practices.** Bloomsbury Academic.
   ISBN: 978-1-4742-4199-4
   DOI: 10.5040/9781474241991
   - First book-length scholarly analysis of critical design, distinguishing associative, speculative, and critical strands.

## Design Fiction

5. **Sterling, B. (2005). Shaping Things.** MIT Press.
   ISBN: 978-0-262-69326-7
   - Introduced the concept of design fiction as a practice at the intersection of design and science fiction; coined "design fiction."

6. **Bleecker, J. (2009). Design Fiction: A Short Essay on Design, Science, Fact and Fiction.** Near Future Laboratory.
   URL: https://blog.nearfuturelaboratory.com/2009/03/17/design-fiction-a-short-essay-on-design-science-fact-and-fiction/
   - Influential essay that formalized design fiction as a practice; emphasized "diegetic prototypes."

7. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2015). Back to the Future: 10 Years of Design Fiction.** *Proceedings of British HCI '15*, 210–211.
   DOI: 10.1145/2783446.2783592
   - Retrospective on a decade of design fiction; proposed taxonomies for design fiction forms.

8. **Lindley, J., & Coulton, P. (2016). Pushing the Limits of Design Fiction: The Case for Fictional Research Papers.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 4032–4043.
   DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858446
   - Extended design fiction into the form of fictional academic papers.

9. **Blythe, M. (2014). Research through Design Fiction: Narrative in Real and Imaginary Abstracts.** *Proceedings of CHI '14*, 703–712.
   DOI: 10.1145/2556288.2557098
   - Explored design fiction as a research method using imaginary conference abstracts.

10. **Tanenbaum, T. J. (2014). Design Fictional Interactions: Why HCI Should Care about Stories.** *Interactions*, 21(5), 22–23.
    DOI: 10.1145/2648414
    - Argued for narrative and fiction as first-class concerns in HCI research.

11. **Kirby, D. (2010). The Future is Now: Diegetic Prototypes and the Role of Popular Films in Generating Real-World Technological Development.** *Social Studies of Science*, 40(1), 41–70.
    DOI: 10.1177/0306312709338325
    - Introduced "diegetic prototypes" — fictional technologies presented within narratives as real; foundational for design fiction theory.

12. **Lindley, J., Sharma, D., & Potts, R. (2014). Anticipatory Ethnography: Design Fiction as an Input to Design Ethnography.** *Proceedings of Ethnographic Praxis in Industry Conference*, 237–253.
    DOI: 10.1111/1559-8918.01030
    - Combined design fiction with ethnographic methods.

## Critical Design and Critical Making

13. **Bardzell, J., & Bardzell, S. (2013). What is "Critical" about Critical Design?** *Proceedings of CHI '13*, 3297–3306.
    DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451
    - Analyzed the theoretical commitments of critical design from a humanities perspective.

14. **Bardzell, S. (2010). Feminist HCI: Taking Stock and Outlining an Agenda for Design.** *Proceedings of CHI '10*, 1301–1310.
    DOI: 10.1145/1753326.1753521
    - Foundational paper for feminist approaches to HCI; intersects with critical design through its focus on values and power.

15. **Bardzell, J., Bardzell, S., & Blythe, M. A. (2018). Critical Theory and Interaction Design.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-03727-5
    - Collected volume on the intersection of critical theory and design; key reference for theorizing critical design.

16. **Ratto, M. (2011). Critical Making: Conceptual and Material Studies in Technology and Social Life.** *The Information Society*, 27(4), 252–260.
    DOI: 10.1080/01972243.2011.583819
    - Introduced "critical making" — combining critical thinking with material production.

17. **DiSalvo, C. (2012). Adversarial Design.** MIT Press.
    ISBN: 978-0-262-01738-1
    DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/8732.001.0001
    - Proposed adversarial design as a political practice; artifacts as means of agonistic contestation.

18. **DiSalvo, C. (2009). Design and the Construction of Publics.** *Design Issues*, 25(1), 48–63.
    DOI: 10.1162/desi.2009.25.1.48
    - Connected design to political theory (Dewey, Latour); design as a means of constructing publics around issues.

## Speculative Design Practice and Critique

19. **Auger, J. (2013). Speculative Design: Crafting the Speculation.** *Digital Creativity*, 24(1), 11–35.
    DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2013.767276
    - Analyzed speculative design methods and their effectiveness; proposed craft of speculation.

20. **Auger, J., & Loizeau, J. (2009). Alternative Presents and Speculative Futures.** In *Negotiating Futures — Design Fiction*, Swiss Design Network.
    URL: https://www.auger-loizeau.com/
    - Practice-based exploration of speculative design through designed objects.

21. **Michael, M. (2012). "What Are We Busy Doing?": Engaging the Idiot.** *Science, Technology, & Human Values*, 37(5), 528–554.
    DOI: 10.1177/0162243911428624
    - Introduced the "idiot" as a figure for speculative thought in STS/design, influential for speculative design theory.

22. **Mazé, R. (2007). Occupying Time: Design, Technology, and the Form of Interaction.** PhD thesis, Blekinge Institute of Technology.
    ISBN: 978-91-7295-108-7
    URL: https://www.diva-portal.org/smash/record.jsf?pid=diva2%3A837485
    - Influential thesis bridging critical design and temporal interaction design.

23. **Mazé, R., & Redström, J. (2009). Difficult Forms: Critical Practices of Design and Research.** *Research Design Journal*, 1, 28–39.
    DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2009.11643285
    - Explored "difficult forms" in design research — objects that resist easy categorization.

24. **Tonkinwise, C. (2015). Just Design: Being Dogmatic about Defining Speculative Critical Design Future Fiction.** Online essay.
    URL: https://medium.com/@camerontw/just-design-b1f97cb3996f
    - Influential critique and taxonomy of the distinctions between speculative, critical, and discursive design.

25. **Prado de O. Martins, L., & Oliveira, P. (2014). Questioning the "Critical" in Speculative & Critical Design.** Medium.
    URL: https://medium.com/a-parede/questioning-the-critical-in-speculative-critical-design-5a355cac2ca4
    - Prominent critique of SCD's Eurocentrism, privilege, and limited engagement with marginalized perspectives.

## Experiential Futures and Worldbuilding

26. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD thesis, University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.
    URL: https://www.stuartcandy.com/thesis
    - Foundational work on experiential futures — designing immersive futures scenarios.

27. **Candy, S., & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136–153.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
    - Extended experiential futures methodology with a detailed case study.

28. **Coulton, P., Lindley, J., & Cooper, R. (2018). The Little Book of Design Fiction for the Internet of Things.** Lancaster University.
    URL: https://www.petrashub.org/the-little-book-of-design-fiction-for-the-internet-of-things/
    - Practical guide to applying design fiction to IoT contexts.

29. **Wong, R. Y., & Khovanskaya, V. (2018). Speculative Design in HCI: From Corporate Imaginations to Critical Orientations.** In *New Directions in Third Wave HCI*, Springer, 175–202.
    DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-73356-2_11
    - Mapped the spectrum of speculative design in HCI from corporate to critical orientations.

30. **Elsden, C., Chatting, D., Durrant, A. C., Garbett, A., Nissen, B., Vines, J., & Kirk, D. S. (2017). On Speculative Enactments.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 5386–5399.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503
    - Introduced "speculative enactments" as a method combining design fiction with user study.

31. **Lindley, J., Coulton, P., & Sturdee, M. (2017). Implications for Adoption.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 265–277.
    DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025742
    - Satirical design fiction paper critiquing HCI's "implications for design" convention.

32. **Galloway, A., & Caudwell, C. (2019). Speculative Design as Research Method: From Answers to Questions and "Staying with the Trouble."** In *Undesign: Critical Practices at the Intersection of Art and Design*, Routledge, 85–96.
    DOI: 10.4324/9781315526379-8
    - Positioned speculative design as a research methodology using Haraway's "staying with the trouble."

33. **Ward, M., & Wilkie, A. (2009). Made in Criticalland: Designing Matters of Concern.** *Networks of Design: Proceedings of the 2008 Annual International Conference of the DRS*, 118–128.
    URL: https://dl.designresearchsociety.org/drs-conference-papers/drs2008/researchpapers/83/
    - Analyzed the social and institutional dynamics of critical design practice.

## Theorizing Alternative Designs (2014–2026)

34. **Pierce, J., Sengers, P., Hirsch, T., Jenkins, T., Gaver, W., & DiSalvo, C. (2015). Expanding and Refining Design and Criticality in HCI.** *Proceedings of CHI '15*, 2083–2092.
    DOI: 10.1145/2702123.2702438
    - Panel paper expanding design criticality in HCI beyond Dunne & Raby's framing.

35. **Pierce, J. (2021). In Tension with Progression: Grasping the Frictional Tendencies of Speculative, Critical, and other Alternative Designs.** *Proceedings of CHI '21*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3411764.3445406
    - **Major theoretical paper.** Develops concepts of *progressional design* (converging toward production) vs. *frictional design* (resisting progression). Introduces *design as prefiguration* and 5 frictional tendencies: diverging, opposing, accelerating, counterfactualizing, analogizing. Provides the first comprehensive theory unifying speculative, critical, and alternative designs under a single framework. Highly cited — spawned significant follow-up work.

36. **Cardenas Cordova, D., Kelly, N., & Rezayan, L. (2025). A systematic literature review of the speculative design process and a proposed framework for speculative design.** *Design Science*.
    DOI: 10.1017/dsj.2025.10030
    - Systematic review of the speculative design *process* — complementary to our genealogical mapping. Proposes a framework.

37. **Celik, A., & Kaya, C. (2025). Reviewing the expansion in Design Futuring Research: Emergent concepts around Speculative Design and Design Fiction.** *Futures*.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2025.103615
    - Reviews design futuring's expansion, connecting speculative design and design fiction to futures studies.

38. **Snow, D., Linehan, C., Robinson, S., Gómez Ortega, A., Lindley, J., McDermott, F., Cowan, B.R., & Barry, M. (2025). Six Degrees of Speculation: Towards a Taxonomy of Temporality.** *Proceedings of DIS '25*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3715668.3734169
    - Taxonomy of temporal dimensions in speculative design. Joe Lindley (Observatory) co-author — connects Observatory work to academic taxonomy-building.

39. **Eschrich, J., McMullen, C., & Sterman, S. (2025). Speculative Design in Spiraling Time: Methods and Indigenous HCI.** arXiv preprint.
    DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2506.10229
    - Decolonial speculative design through Indigenous temporal frameworks — extends the Prado/Schultz critiques.

40. **Grønbæk, J.E., Klokmose, C., & Hornbæk, K. (2026). How Do Future Visions Shape the Field of Human-Computer Interaction?** *Proceedings of CHI '26*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3772318.3791038
    - CHI '26 full paper mapping how future visions shape HCI — methodologically close to our genealogy work.

41. **Frauenberger, C., et al. (2026). Playful Frictional Design: A Design Space Grounded in Playfulness, Friction, and the Everyday.** *CHI '26 Extended Abstracts*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3772363.3799290
    - Extends Pierce's frictional design into playfulness. Frauenberger (entanglement HCI) connecting to Pierce's framework.

## Quality and Legitimacy of Speculation (2023–2026)

42. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Neuhaus, R., Dörrenbächer, J., Großkopp, S., Theofanou-Fülbier, D., & Hassenzahl, M. (2023). Design Fiction in a Corporate Setting – a Case Study.** *Proceedings of DIS '23*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3563657.3596126
    - Explored potentials and limits of speculative methods (design fiction) applied in corporate/industry contexts, beyond academia.

43. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Dörrenbächer, J., & Hassenzahl, M. (2025). The Quality of Speculation – A Scoping Review.** *Proceedings of DIS '25*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3715336.3735794
    - **Key paper:** First systematic scoping review addressing what constitutes "good" or "bad" speculation. Developed a descriptive taxonomy of quality for speculative design. Directly addresses the methodological vagueness critique that runs through the SCD lineage. Methodologically parallel to our genealogy project.

44. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Dörrenbächer, J., Elsden, C., Auger, J., Pierce, J., Wong, R. Y., & Hassenzahl, M. (2026). The Quality of Speculation: Common Ground for Speculative Design in Human-Computer Interaction?** *CHI '26 Extended Abstracts*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3772363.3778760
    - Workshop paper building on the DIS '25 taxonomy. Brings together researchers to collectively examine how quality is constructed and negotiated across speculative design practices and contexts. Notable co-authors: Chris Elsden (Speculative Enactments), James Auger (speculative design practice pioneer), James Pierce, Richmond Wong.

45. **Zhu, L., Chao, C., & Fu, Z. (2024). How HCI Integrates Speculative Thinking to Envision Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 28(4), 89–103.
    - Recent mapping of how speculative thinking has been integrated into HCI research — another genealogical/mapping paper.

## Summary

Speculative and critical design branched from RtD through Dunne's critical design (1999), was popularized via Dunne & Raby (2013), and diversified into design fiction (Sterling, Bleecker, Lindley), critical making (Ratto), adversarial design (DiSalvo), and experiential futures (Candy). Key tensions include: who gets to speculate, what counts as "critical," and whether provocation constitutes research.

**2023–2026 update:** The "Quality of Speculation" thread (Ringfort-Felner et al.) represents the field's most systematic attempt to address speculative design's legitimacy crisis through a quality taxonomy. This, combined with Zhu et al.'s (2024) mapping of speculative thinking in HCI, signals that the field is actively trying to consolidate and codify — exactly the kind of methodological self-reflection our genealogy paper aims to support.
