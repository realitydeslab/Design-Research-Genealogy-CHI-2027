# Literature Review: Recent Trends in Design Research Methods (2024–2026)

## Overview

The period 2024–2026 reveals five macro-trends reshaping design research methodology: (1) a **self-codification wave** — systematic and scoping reviews mapping sub-fields from within; (2) **generative AI disruption** — GenAI entering speculative, participatory, and qualitative methods; (3) **more-than-human acceleration** — moving from theory to practice and institutionalization; (4) **counter-movement consolidation** — unmaking gains its own TOCHI synthesis; and (5) **handbook maturation** — new editions signal established sub-fields. These trends collectively suggest the field is simultaneously expanding its boundaries (AI, multispecies) and consolidating its knowledge base (reviews, handbooks, taxonomies).

---

## Trend 1: The Self-Codification Wave

The field is actively reviewing and codifying itself. Multiple systematic reviews and scoping reviews map sub-fields that were previously documented only through individual papers.

### Participatory Design

1. **Qi, X., & Yu, J. (2025). Participatory Design in Human-Computer Interaction: Cases, Characteristics, and Lessons.** *Proceedings of CHI '25*. ACM.
   DOI: 10.1145/3706598.3713436
   - Systematic review of 185 SIGCHI papers (2014–2024). Maps PD's evolution in HCI: who participates, what methods are used, what outcomes result. The most comprehensive empirical mapping of PD practice in a decade.

### Speculative Design Quality

2. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Dörrenbächer, J., & Hassenzahl, M. (2025). The Quality of Speculation — A Scoping Review.** *Proceedings of DIS '25*, 2373–2394.
   DOI: 10.1145/3715336.3735794
   - Scoping review of 63 papers. Develops a descriptive taxonomy for evaluating speculative design quality. Addresses the long-standing criticism that speculative design lacks evaluation criteria.

3. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Dörrenbächer, J., & Hassenzahl, M. (2025). Otherware and the Not-Yet: A Methodological Investigation into Speculative Design for HCI.** *Proceedings of DIS '25*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3715668.3735630
   - Companion paper. Proposes "otherware" — speculative artifacts that are neither prototypes nor provocations — as a distinct category requiring its own evaluation approach.

### Unmaking

4. **Song, K. W., Sabie, S., Jackson, S., Lindström, K., Paulos, E., Ståhl, Å., & Wakkary, R. (2025). Unmaking and HCI: Techniques, Technologies, Materials, and Philosophies beyond Making.** *ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction*, 31(6).
   DOI: 10.1145/3689047
   - Comprehensive synthesis mapping unmaking across HCI. Establishes unmaking as a counter-movement with its own techniques (biodegradation, removal, repair), materials, and philosophical commitments. TOCHI publication signals field-level recognition.

### Autoethnography in HCI

5. **Kaltenhauser, A., Stefanidi, E., & Schöning, J. (2024). Playing with Perspectives and Unveiling the Autoethnographic Kaleidoscope in HCI — A Literature Review of Autoethnographies.** *Proceedings of CHI '24*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3613904.3642355
   - Systematic literature review of HCI autoethnographies. Confirmed rapid growth. Categorized by topic, method, and contribution type. The most comprehensive survey to date.

### Design-Based Research in HCI

6. **Design-Based Research in Human-Computer Interaction: A Scoping Review. (2025).** *Interacting with Computers*. Oxford University Press.
   DOI: 10.1093/iwc/iwaf030
   - Scoping review distinguishing design-based research (DBR) from RtD in HCI contexts. Maps methodological boundaries between related approaches.

## Trend 2: Generative AI Disruption

Generative AI is entering design research methods — not just as a design tool, but as a methodological disruptor that changes how research is conducted.

### GenAI in Design Practice

7. **Naqvi, S. M., He, R., & Kaur, H. (2025). Catalyst for Creativity or a Hollow Trend?: A Cross-Level Perspective on The Role of Generative AI in Design.** *Proceedings of CHI '25*.
   - Critical examination of GenAI's role across design practice levels. Questions whether GenAI genuinely transforms design methodology or represents surface-level adoption.

### GenAI + Speculative Design

8. **Tao, H., & Vyas, D. (2025). "Housing Diversity Means Diverse Housing": Blending Generative AI into Speculative Design in Rural Co-Housing Communities.** *Proceedings of CHI '25*, Article 527.
   DOI: 10.1145/3706598.3713906
   - Uses GenAI within speculative design process for community co-housing. Demonstrates blending AI-generated imagery into participatory speculation.

9. **Ardia (2024). Speculative Design with Generative AI: Applying Stable Diffusion and ChatGPT to Imagining Climate Change Futures.** *11th International Conference on Digital and Interactive Arts (ARTECH '24)*.
   DOI: 10.1145/3632776.3632827
   - GenAI tools (Stable Diffusion, ChatGPT) as materials for speculative design about climate futures.

### AI-Assisted Qualitative Analysis

10. **Chatzichristos, G. (2025). Qualitative Research in the Era of AI: A Return to Positivism or a New Paradigm?** *International Journal of Qualitative Methods*.
    DOI: 10.1177/16094069251337583
    - Questions whether AI-assisted qualitative analysis pushes researchers back toward positivism — directly relevant for RtD's reflexive knowledge claims.

11. **Nguyen, D. C., & Welch, C. (2026). Generative Artificial Intelligence in Qualitative Data Analysis: Analyzing — Or Just Chatting?** *Organizational Research Methods*.
    DOI: 10.1177/10944281251377154
    - Examines RAG architectures for qualitative coding. Asks whether GenAI genuinely analyzes or merely converses — epistemological question for design research.

### Human-AI Co-Creation

12. **Exploring Collaboration Patterns and Strategies in Human-AI Co-creation through the Lens of Agency. (2025).** *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction* (CSCW).
    DOI: 10.1145/3757594
    - Systematic review of agency in human-AI co-creation. Maps collaboration patterns across HCI venues. Relevant to PD + AI intersection.

13. **Generative AI-enhanced Human-AI Collaborative Conceptual Design: A Systematic Literature Review. (2025).** *Design Studies*.
    - SLR mapping GenAI's role in collaborative conceptual design. Published in *Design Studies*, the field's premier methodology journal.

### Participatory Design + LLMs

14. **AI-assisted Participatory Design: A Study on the Performance of Large Language Models in Community Renewal. (2025).** *Journal of Asian Architecture and Building Engineering*.
    DOI: 10.1080/13467581.2025.2587376
    - LLMs performing participatory design tasks in community planning contexts. Raises questions about AI as participant vs. tool.

15. **Leveraging LLM-based Multi-agent Simulations to Boost Participatory Design Education. (2025).** *Sustainable Cities and Society*.
    - MAPS framework: LLM agents simulating stakeholders in PD education. Introduces multi-agent simulation as PD pedagogical method.

### Workshop: GenAI + HCI Methodology

16. **Muller, M. et al. (2025). GenAICHI 2025: Generative AI and HCI at CHI 2025.** *CHI '25 Extended Abstracts*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3706599.3707213
    - The main CHI workshop on GenAI + HCI methodology. Signals institutional recognition of AI as methodological concern.

## Trend 3: More-than-Human Acceleration

The more-than-human turn is moving from theoretical framing to practical methods and institutional recognition.

17. **Giaccardi, E., Redström, J., & Nicenboim, I. (2024). The Making(s) of More-Than-Human Design: Introduction to the Special Issue on More-Than-Human Design and HCI.** *Human–Computer Interaction*.
    DOI: 10.1080/07370024.2024.2353357
    - Special issue introduction. Signals journal-level recognition of MtHD as a distinct methodological orientation in HCI.

18. **Tomico, O. et al. (2024). Seeding a Repository of Methods-To-Be for Nature-Entangled Design Research.** *Proceedings of DIS '24*, 1101–1115.
    DOI: 10.1145/3643834.3660745
    - "Methods-to-be" — emerging, not-yet-codified methods for nature-entangled research. Meta-methodological contribution: how do you document methods that are still forming?

19. **Poikolainen Rosén, A., Salovaara, A., Botero, A., & Søndergaard, M. L. J. (2025). Introducing More-Than-Human Design in Practice.** *ACM Interactions*, XXXII(2).
    DOI: 10.1145/3712714
    - Forum piece bringing MtHD from theory to practice. Accompanied by a Routledge book: *More-Than-Human Design in Practice* (2025, ISBN 9781032741208).

## Five Macro-Trends Summarized

| Trend | Evidence | Implication for Genealogy |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-codification** | 6+ systematic/scoping reviews mapping sub-fields (PD, speculation, unmaking, autoethnography, DBR) | The field is building cumulative knowledge — intermediate-level knowledge debate is being resolved through practice |
| **GenAI disruption** | 10+ papers on AI entering speculative, participatory, and qualitative methods | New branching point: every existing lineage now has an "AI-augmented" variant emerging |
| **More-than-human acceleration** | Special issue (HCI journal), methods repository (DIS '24), practice book (Routledge '25) | Moving from Layer 9 paradigm to Layer 2 methodology — MtHD is becoming a method, not just a lens |
| **Counter-movement consolidation** | Unmaking TOCHI synthesis; removal as method | Counter-movements are being absorbed into the mainstream genealogy |
| **Handbook maturation** | New PD handbook (Routledge '25), MtHD practice book | Sub-fields are crystallizing — the genealogy captures a moment of consolidation |

## Connections to Genealogy Lineages

- **→ Intermediate-Level Knowledge:** The self-codification wave *is* the field producing intermediate-level knowledge (taxonomies, scoping reviews, quality frameworks). The debate about what kind of knowledge RtD produces is being answered empirically.
- **→ Participatory Design:** Qi & Yu (2025) maps PD's current state; the new handbook (Smith et al. 2025) codifies it; participatory AI (entries 14–15, 21) extends it into AI contexts.
- **→ Speculative Design:** Ringfort-Felner et al. (2025) addresses the quality crisis; GenAI enters as a new material for speculation (entries 8–9).
- **→ First-Person Methods:** Kaltenhauser et al. (2024) confirms autoethnography's growth; AI-assisted qualitative analysis (entries 10–11) threatens the reflexive stance.
- **→ More-than-Human Design:** The most active area — moving from theory to method (entries 17–19).
- **→ Unmaking:** Song et al. (2025) consolidates the counter-movement into TOCHI.
- **→ Design Methods Movement:** The self-codification wave mirrors the original design methods movement's attempt to systematize design — but now the object of systematization is the methods themselves.
