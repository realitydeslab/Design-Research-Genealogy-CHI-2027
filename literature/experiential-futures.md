# Literature Review: Experiential Futures & Speculative Enactments

## Overview

Experiential Futures and Speculative Enactments represent a lineage of design research methods that make speculation *lived* — moving beyond artifacts, scenarios, and narratives to create immersive situations where people experience possible futures firsthand. Where speculative design produces objects for contemplation and design fiction produces narratives for discussion, experiential futures and speculative enactments produce **situations for inhabiting**.

This lineage has two primary strands that emerged independently but converge: (1) **Experiential Futures** (Candy 2010), rooted in professional futures studies and foresight — bringing scenarios to life through immersive, embodied encounters; and (2) **Speculative Enactments** (Elsden et al. 2017), rooted in HCI/design research — creating situations where people perform speculative scenarios with real stakes, generating data about how they would actually behave with imagined technologies.

The genealogical significance is that this lineage resolves a key critique of speculative/critical design: that gallery-based provocations produce only aesthetic responses, not actionable insights about how people would actually live with future technologies. By making speculation *experiential* and *enacted*, this lineage bridges speculative design's provocative intent with RtD's demand for empirical grounding.

## Strand 1: Experiential Futures (Candy)

### Foundational Works

1. **Candy, S. (2010). The Futures of Everyday Life: Politics and the Design of Experiential Scenarios.** PhD Dissertation, University of Hawaii at Manoa.
   URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/305280378
   - **The foundational text.** Defines experiential futures as the practice of bringing scenarios to life through tangible artifacts, immersive situations, and embodied encounters. Grounded in professional foresight (Dator's alternative futures) but oriented toward design practice. Documented the Hawaii 2050 project: four immersive scenarios for hundreds of policymakers and citizens.

2. **Candy, S. & Dunagan, J. (2017). Designing an Experiential Scenario: The People Who Vanished.** *Futures*, 86, 136-153.
   DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2016.05.006
   - Detailed case study of designing an experiential scenario. Documents the design process, materiality, and situated encounter. Bridges futures studies methods with design practice.

3. **Candy, S. (2016). The Experiential Turn.** Blog post / essay.
   URL: https://futuryst.blogspot.com/2016/12/the-experiential-turn.html
   - Frames experiential futures as the central emerging challenge for foresight: "the central emerging challenge for foresight practitioners has less to do with generating and broadcasting ideas about the future than with designing circumstances or situations in which the collective intelligence and imagination of a community can come forth."

### The Thing From The Future

4. **Candy, S. & Watson, J. (2015). The Thing From The Future.** Situation Lab. Published in APF *Compass* Methods Anthology.
   URL: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/312016855
   - Award-winning card game for experiential futures and imagination. Four card types (arc, terrain, object, mood) combine to generate prompts for imagining future artifacts. Widely adopted in design education, workshops, and foresight practice. Published by Situation Lab (Candy & Watson).

5. **Candy, S. & Watson, J. (2018). Gaming Futures Literacy: The Thing From The Future.** In R. Miller (Ed.), *Transforming the Future: Anticipation in the 21st Century*, 233-246. UNESCO/Routledge.
   - Positions The Thing From The Future within UNESCO's Futures Literacy framework. Shows how game mechanics can scaffold futures imagination.

### Experiential Futures Ladder

6. **Candy, S. & Kornet, K. (2019). Turning Foresight Inside Out: An Introduction to Ethnographic Experiential Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 23(3), 3-22.
   DOI: 10.6531/JFS.201903_23(3).0002
   - Introduces the "experiential futures ladder" — a framework showing how scenarios move from abstract (setting/scenario) to concrete (situation/stuff). Each rung closer to the ground = more experiential, more embodied, more designerly. Connects experiential futures to ethnographic practice.

7. **Candy, S. (2018). U.S. Earth Force.** Experiential scenario, CMU Situation Lab / IFTF / World Bank CIF.
   - Landmark experiential scenario created for climate futures. Participants entered an immersive environment depicting a specific climate future. Demonstrated experiential futures at institutional scale.

## Strand 2: Speculative Enactments (Elsden et al.)

### Foundational Work

8. **Elsden, C., Chatting, D., Durrant, A.C., Garbett, A., Nissen, B., Vines, J. & Kirk, D.S. (2017). On Speculative Enactments.** *Proceedings of CHI '17*, 5386-5399.
   DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503
   - **The foundational HCI paper.** Defines speculative enactments as situations where participants seriously invest in a speculative scenario to the level that their responses become meaningful data. Distinguishes from design fiction (narrative), speculative design (artifact), and technology probes (functional). Key criterion: creating "conditions in which an abstract speculation becomes real and tractable, to a level that participants seriously invest in the enactment." Provides guidelines for designing speculative enactments.

9. **Elsden, C., Durrant, A.C., Chatting, D., Green, D. & Kirk, D.S. (2017). Abacus Datagraphy: A Speculative Enactment.** *Proceedings of the 3rd Biennial Research Through Design (RTD) Conference*.
   - Companion paper presenting a specific speculative enactment: participants used a data-tracking abacus to record their daily activities, enacting a speculative future of manual data tracking. Demonstrates how enactments generate empirical data about speculative scenarios.

### Extensions

10. **[Authors]. (2022). Immersive Speculative Enactments: Bringing Future Scenarios and Technology to Life Using Virtual Reality.** *Proceedings of CHI '22*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3491102.3517492
    - Extends speculative enactments into VR. Uses immersive virtual reality to create speculative scenarios that participants can inhabit spatially. Bridges XR technology with speculative design methods.

11. **Elsden, C., Nissen, B., Garbett, A., Chatting, D., Kirk, D.S. & Vines, J. (2018). Making Sense of Blockchain Applications: A Typology for HCI.** *Proceedings of CHI '18*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3173574.3174032
    - Applied speculative enactment thinking to blockchain technology. Participants enacted blockchain-based systems before they existed, generating insights about user experience of distributed ledger technologies.

## Related Methods: Bridging Concepts

### Research Products (Odom)

12. **Odom, W., Wakkary, R., Lim, Y., Desjardins, A., Hengeveld, B. & Banks, R. (2016). From Research Prototype to Research Product.** *Proceedings of CHI '16*, 2549-2561.
    DOI: 10.1145/2858036.2858167
    - "Research products" — fully functional speculative artifacts deployed in people's homes for weeks/months. The temporal extension of speculative enactments: rather than a single enacted situation, participants live with a speculative artifact over time. Bridges material speculation and experiential futures.

### Speculative Thinking in HCI (Zhu et al.)

13. **Zhu, Y., et al. (2024). How HCI Integrates Speculative Thinking to Envision Futures.** *Journal of Futures Studies*, 28(4).
    - Systematic review of speculative methods in HCI. Maps the landscape including design fiction, speculative design, experiential futures, and speculative enactments. Positions them as a "spectrum of speculation" from abstract (scenarios) to embodied (enactments).

### Design Fiction as World Building

14. **Coulton, P. & Lindley, J. (2017). Design Fiction as World Building.** *Proceedings of RTD '17*.
    DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.4746964
    - World building creates the internally-consistent fictional world; experiential futures and speculative enactments make that world *inhabitable*. Design fiction provides the narrative scaffold; experiential futures provides the embodied encounter.

### Speculative Enactments + Quality of Speculation

15. **Ringfort-Felner, R., Dörrenbächer, J. & Hassenzahl, M. (2025). The Quality of Speculation — A Scoping Review.** *Proceedings of DIS '25*.
    DOI: 10.1145/3715336.3735794
    - In their quality taxonomy, Ringfort-Felner et al. note that speculative enactments produce "moments where people grapple with the implications of a designed speculation in a way that is personally meaningful." Positions enactments as a quality indicator for speculation.

## The "Experiential Futures Ladder" (Candy & Kornet 2019)

A key framework for the genealogy:

```
ABSTRACT                              CONCRETE
   ↓                                     ↓
Setting → Scenario → Situation → Stuff
   ↓         ↓          ↓          ↓
 world    narrative   immersive   tangible
 building  fiction    enactment   artifact

← Futures Studies                Design →
← Foresight methods          Experiential →
```

- **Setting** = the world-level context (world building)
- **Scenario** = a narrative within that setting (design fiction)
- **Situation** = an immersive encounter with the scenario (experiential futures / speculative enactments)
- **Stuff** = tangible artifacts from the future (future artifacts, research products)

This ladder maps directly onto our genealogy: World Building → Design Fiction → Experiential Futures/Speculative Enactments → Material Speculation/Research Products, with each step becoming more embodied and concrete.

## Connections to Other Lineages

| Connected Lineage | Nature of Connection |
|---|---|
| **Speculative Design** | Experiential futures and speculative enactments are methods for making speculation *lived* rather than merely contemplated. They address speculative design's core critique: "but does anyone actually engage with it?" |
| **Design Fiction** | Design fiction provides narrative; experiential futures provides embodied encounter. Candy's ladder shows them as adjacent rungs: scenario → situation. |
| **World Building** | World building creates the fictional world; experiential futures makes it inhabitable. Coulton & Lindley (2017) + Candy (2010) are complementary. |
| **Foresight** | Candy bridges foresight (Dator, Voros) and design. Experiential futures is foresight made designerly — moving from abstract scenarios to tangible situations. |
| **Design Futuring** | Fry's design futuring + Candy's experiential futures share sustainability orientation. Candy's Hawaii 2050 was a sustainability futures project. |
| **Material Speculation** | Odom's research products are the temporal extension of speculative enactments — living with speculation for weeks rather than enacting it for hours. |
| **Artist-led Research** | Experiential futures share the "immersive situation" orientation of Benford's performance-led research. Both create designed experiences; Candy from foresight, Benford from art. |
| **Participatory Design** | Experiential futures are inherently participatory — participants are co-creators of meaning within the designed situation. Candy's work at Hawaii 2050 was explicitly democratic. |
| **Provotypes** | Provotypes provoke discussion through artifacts; speculative enactments provoke through lived situations. Both are participatory and oriented toward generating reactions. |
| **Anticipatory Ethnography** | Lindley's anticipatory ethnography conducts ethnographic analysis of fictional futures; speculative enactments create situations where ethnographic analysis of enacted futures becomes possible. |

## Implications for the Genealogy

**Position:** Experiential Futures / Speculative Enactments sit at the junction between Foresight (professional futures studies) and the Speculative Branch (speculative design, design fiction). They represent the **experiential turn** in speculation — the moment where speculation moves from objects-to-be-looked-at to situations-to-be-inhabited.

**Branching points:**
1. **Foresight → Experiential Futures** (Candy 2010): scenarios become immersive situations. The foresight tradition gains a designerly, embodied dimension.
2. **Speculative Design → Speculative Enactments** (Elsden 2017): gallery provocations become participatory encounters. The speculative tradition gains an empirical, HCI-research dimension.
3. **Both → Research Products** (Odom 2016): enactments become long-term deployments. The experiential/enacted becomes the lived-with.
