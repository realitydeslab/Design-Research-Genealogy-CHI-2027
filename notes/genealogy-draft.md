# Design Research Genealogy: Draft Mapping

## Overview

This document maps the genealogical relationships between design research methodologies — how they emerged from, influenced, diverged from, and converged with each other. The genealogy spans from 1983 (Schön's *Reflective Practitioner*) through 2026, covering the major lineages identified in our literature review.

---

## Root Ancestors (Pre-1993)

These works predate the formal taxonomy of design research methodologies but provide the intellectual soil from which all lineages grow:

- **Schön (1983)** — *The Reflective Practitioner*: Established reflection-in-action as a mode of professional knowing. Foundation for all practice-based design research. ISBN: 978-0465068784
- **Scandinavian PD movement (1970s–80s)** — Workplace democracy + cooperative systems design. Ehn (1988) *Work-Oriented Design of Computer Artifacts*. DOI: 10.1201/9780203743461. Bødker (1991) *Through the Interface*. Greenbaum & Kyng (1991) *Design at Work*. ISBN: 978-0805806113
- **Cross (1982)** — "Designerly Ways of Knowing." *Design Studies* 3(4), 221–227. DOI: 10.1016/0142-694X(82)90040-0. Argued design is a distinct intellectual culture with its own epistemology.
- **Archer (1995)** — Parallel UK articulation of research in/through/for design. URL: https://researchonline.rca.ac.uk/384/

---

## The Trunk: Frayling's Taxonomy (1993)

**Frayling, C. (1993). Research in Art and Design.** *Royal College of Art Research Papers* 1(1). URL: https://researchonline.rca.ac.uk/384/

The originating taxonomy: research *into*, *through*, and *for* art and design. This single paper is the common ancestor for virtually all design research methodologies that followed. Every lineage either builds on or reacts against it.

**Branching point:** Frayling's "research through design" became the name of an entire movement, but his other categories (*into* and *for*) also spawned distinct research programs.

## Major Lineages and Branching Points

### Lineage 1: Participatory Design (1970s → present)

**Timeline:** 1970s Scandinavian origins → 1988 Ehn (DOI: 10.1201/9780203743461) → 1991 Greenbaum & Kyng → 1993 Schuler & Namioka (*Participatory Design*, ISBN: 978-0805809510) → 1999 cultural probes: Gaver, Dunne & Pacenti (DOI: 10.1145/291224.291235) → 2008 co-design: Sanders & Stappers (DOI: 10.1080/15710880701875068) → 2010 design things: Ehn (DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/8262.003.0006) → 2013 Simonsen & Robertson, *Routledge International Handbook of Participatory Design* (ISBN: 978-0415694407) → 2019 design justice: Costanza-Chock (DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001) → 2020 more-than-human participation: Akama et al. (DOI: 10.1145/3357236.3395590)

**Key branching points:**
- **1999:** Cultural probes (Gaver, Dunne & Pacenti, DOI: 10.1145/291224.291235) branch participatory methods into more exploratory, aesthetic territory → eventually feeds into RtD and speculative design
- **2008:** Sanders & Stappers codify "co-design" (DOI: 10.1080/15710880701875068) as distinct from traditional PD → broader adoption outside Scandinavian tradition
- **2010:** Ehn's "design things" bridges PD with ANT/STS → seeds more-than-human participatory design
- **2019–20:** Costanza-Chock's *Design Justice* (DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001) + Akama et al.'s more-than-human participation → PD branches toward social justice and posthumanism

**Descendants:** Co-design, design justice, infrastructuring, more-than-human participation

---

### Lineage 2: Research through Design (1993 → present)

**Timeline:** 1993 Frayling → 2003 Fallman (DOI: 10.1145/642611.642652) → 2007 Zimmerman, Forlizzi & Evenson, CHI formalization (DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240704) → 2008 Zimmerman & Forlizzi (DOI: 10.1007/978-1-84800-136-7_10) → 2010 Zimmerman, Stolterman & Forlizzi (DOI: 10.1145/1858171.1858228) → 2011 Koskinen et al. *Design Research through Practice* (DOI: 10.1016/C2010-0-65896-2) → 2012 Gaver & Bowers, annotated portfolios (DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889) → 2012 Höök & Löwgren, strong concepts (DOI: 10.1145/2362364.2362371) → 2017 Redström, *Making Design Theory* (ISBN: 978-0262036658) → 2019 Frauenberger, entanglement HCI (DOI: 10.1145/3364998)

**Key branching points:**
- **2007:** Zimmerman et al. formalize RtD for HCI (DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240704) → creates the "HCI-flavored RtD" branch distinct from art/design school RtD
- **2011:** Koskinen et al.'s *lab/field/showroom* taxonomy (DOI: 10.1016/C2010-0-65896-2) → constructive design research as a broader umbrella
- **2012:** Gaver & Bowers' annotated portfolios (DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889) + Höök & Löwgren's strong concepts (DOI: 10.1145/2362364.2362371) → two competing models for intermediate knowledge, both still active
- **2017:** Redström's *Making Design Theory* (ISBN: 978-0262036658) → programmatic approach to design theory construction
- **2019:** Frauenberger's entanglement HCI (DOI: 10.1145/3364998) → RtD meets posthumanism/new materialism, bridging to more-than-human design

**Descendants:** Annotated portfolios, strong concepts, concept-driven design research, constructive design research, entanglement HCI

---

### Lineage 3: Critical & Speculative Design (1999 → present)

**Timeline:** 1999 Dunne, *Hertzian Tales* (ISBN: 978-0262541992) → 2001 Dunne & Raby, *Design Noir* (ISBN: 978-3764365660) → 2005 Sterling, *Shaping Things* (ISBN: 978-0262693264) → 2009 Bleecker, "Design Fiction" essay (URL: http://dfrlab.org/designfiction) → 2011 Ratto, critical making (DOI: 10.1080/01972243.2011.583819) → 2012 DiSalvo, *Adversarial Design* (ISBN: 978-0262017381) → 2013 Dunne & Raby, *Speculative Everything* (ISBN: 978-0262019842) → 2013 Bardzell & Bardzell, critique of critical design (DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451) → 2017 Malpass, *Critical Design in Context* (DOI: 10.5040/9781474241991) → 2018 decolonizing critiques: Schultz et al. (DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.05.001)

**Key branching points:**
- **1999–2001:** Dunne's *Hertzian Tales* + *Design Noir* establish critical design as a distinct practice → separates from RtD by emphasizing provocation over problem-solving
- **2005–2009:** Sterling and Bleecker coin and formalize **design fiction** → branches from critical design by emphasizing narrative/diegetic prototypes over gallery objects
- **2011:** Ratto's **critical making** (DOI: 10.1080/01972243.2011.583819) → bridges critical design with material practice; making as critical thinking
- **2012:** DiSalvo's **adversarial design** (ISBN: 978-0262017381) → politicizes critical design through agonistic theory (Mouffe)
- **2013:** *Speculative Everything* (ISBN: 978-0262019842) popularizes speculative design → simultaneously canonizes and crystallizes it, inviting critique
- **2014–18:** Prado & Oliveira; Schultz et al. (DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.05.001) → **decolonizing critiques** challenge SCD's Eurocentrism → leads to decolonial design futures
- **2017:** Malpass (DOI: 10.5040/9781474241991) distinguishes associative, speculative, and critical design → first systematic internal taxonomy

**Descendants:** Design fiction, critical making, adversarial design, speculative enactments, decolonial design

---

### Lineage 4: Design Fiction (2005 → present)

**Parent lineage:** Branches from Speculative & Critical Design

**Timeline:** 2005 Sterling, *Shaping Things* (ISBN: 978-0262693264) → 2009 Bleecker → 2010 Kirby, diegetic prototypes (DOI: 10.1177/0306312709338325) → 2014 Blythe, fictional abstracts (DOI: 10.1145/2556288.2557098); Tanenbaum, narrative HCI; Lindley & Sharma → 2015 Lindley & Coulton, decade retrospective (DOI: 10.1145/2783446.2783592) → 2016 Lindley & Coulton, fictional research papers → 2017 Elsden et al., speculative enactments (DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503) → 2019 Coulton & Lindley, IoT design fiction (DOI: 10.1080/14626268.2019.1567775)

**Key branching points:**
- **2010:** Kirby's "diegetic prototypes" (DOI: 10.1177/0306312709338325) grounds design fiction in film studies → gives theoretical legitimacy
- **2014:** Multiple CHI papers → design fiction enters HCI mainstream
- **2017:** Elsden et al.'s "speculative enactments" (DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503) → design fiction merges with user study methods → bridges to participatory futures

**Convergence:** Design fiction increasingly converges with experiential futures (Candy) and participatory methods

---

### Lineage 5: More-than-Human Design (2016 → present)

**Parent lineages:** Draws from posthumanist theory (Haraway, Barad, Braidotti), ANT (Latour), RtD, and PD

**Timeline:** 2005 Latour (ANT) + Verbeek, postphenomenology (DOI: 10.1007/s10699-005-3052-4) → 2016 Giaccardi et al., thing ethnography (DOI: 10.1145/2901790.2901905); Forlano, decentering (DOI: 10.1162/DESI_a_00399) → 2017 Forlano, *Posthumanism and Design* (DOI: 10.1016/j.sheji.2017.08.001); Wakkary et al., Morse Things (DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025956) → 2019 Giaccardi & Redström (DOI: 10.1162/desi_a_00612, published 2020); Clarke et al.; Frauenberger, entanglement HCI (DOI: 10.1145/3364998) → 2020 Akama et al., more-than-human participation (DOI: 10.1145/3357236.3395590) → 2021 Wakkary, *Things We Could Design* (ISBN: 978-0262542999) → 2022 Oogjes & Wakkary, weaving stories (DOI: 10.1145/3532106.3533524)

**Key branching points:**
- **2016–17:** Forlano, Giaccardi, and Wakkary independently articulate more-than-human design → convergent emergence from different philosophical roots
- **2020:** Giaccardi & Redström name "more-than-human design" in *Design Issues* (DOI: 10.1162/desi_a_00612) → crystallization of the field
- **2019:** Frauenberger's entanglement HCI (DOI: 10.1145/3364998) → bridge between RtD and more-than-human → Barad enters HCI
- **2020–21:** Wakkary's book (ISBN: 978-0262542999) + Akama et al.'s more-than-human participation (DOI: 10.1145/3357236.3395590) → branches into both thing-centered and multispecies directions

**Sub-branches:**
- **Thing-centered design:** Giaccardi, Wakkary, Redström → IoT, computational things
- **Multispecies design:** Westerlaken (DOI: 10.1145/3313831.3376468); Mancini, ACI (DOI: 10.1145/2559206.2581137) → animals, ecosystems
- **Posthuman HCI:** Frauenberger (DOI: 10.1145/3364998) → entanglement, agential realism in HCI

---

### Lineage 6: Futuring & Transition Design (2006 → present)

**Parent lineages:** Futures studies (Dator, Bell, Slaughter), design for sustainability, PD

**Timeline:** 2006 Willis, ontological designing (DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514) → 2009 Fry, *Design Futuring* (ISBN: 978-1847882172) → 2010 Candy, experiential futures PhD (DOI: 10.13140/RG.2.1.1840.0248) → 2015 Irwin, transition design (DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829); Manzini, *Design, When Everybody Designs* (ISBN: 978-0262028608) → 2017 Candy & Dunagan (DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2017.03.002) → 2018 Escobar, *Designs for the Pluriverse* (DOI: 10.1215/9780822371090) → 2019 Mazé, politics of design futures (DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.05.002); Candy & Kornet (DOI: 10.1016/j.futures.2019.05.003)

**Key branching points:**
- **2006–09:** Willis (DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514) and Fry establish "ontological designing" and "design futuring" → design itself has futural agency
- **2010:** Candy's experiential futures (DOI: 10.13140/RG.2.1.1840.0248) → makes futures tangible through design; bridges design fiction with foresight
- **2015:** Irwin's transition design (DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829) → ambitious synthesis of sustainability science, systems thinking, and design → CMU school
- **2018:** Escobar's *Designs for the Pluriverse* (DOI: 10.1215/9780822371090) → decolonial design futures → challenges Western futuring dominance

**Convergence with other lineages:**
- Experiential futures ↔ design fiction (shared interest in materializing futures)
- Transition design ↔ PD (shared interest in democratic participation in change)
- Decolonial design ↔ design justice (shared critique of power asymmetries)

### Lineage 7: Feminist HCI & Design Justice (2010 → present)

**Cross-cutting lineage** — not a single genealogical branch but a critical lens that intersects multiple lineages:

**Timeline:** 2010 Bardzell, *Feminist HCI* (DOI: 10.1145/1753326.1753521) → 2013 Bardzell & Bardzell, critical design analysis (DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451) → 2015 Bardzell et al., *Immodest Proposals* → 2019 Harrington et al., equitable PD (DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300233) → 2020 Costanza-Chock, *Design Justice* (DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001)

**Intersections:**
- With RtD: Bardzell et al. (2015) on how RtD generates knowledge
- With critical design: Bardzell & Bardzell (2013, DOI: 10.1145/2470654.2466451) on what "critical" means
- With PD: Harrington et al. (2019, DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300233) on equitable participation
- With futures: Appadurai (2013) on unequal capacity to aspire (DOI: 10.1215/9780822375203)

## Key Cross-Lineage Influences

### Convergences
1. **RtD + Posthumanism → Entanglement HCI** (Frauenberger, 2019; DOI: 10.1145/3364998): RtD's practice-based epistemology meets Barad's agential realism
2. **Design Fiction + Experiential Futures → Speculative Enactments** (Elsden et al., 2017; DOI: 10.1145/3025453.3025503): Narrative speculation meets immersive scenario design
3. **PD + More-than-Human → More-than-Human Participation** (Akama et al., 2020; DOI: 10.1145/3357236.3395590): Democratic participation extended to nonhuman entities
4. **Critical Design + Futures Studies → Decolonial Design** (Escobar, 2018; DOI: 10.1215/9780822371090; Schultz et al., 2018; DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.05.001): Critique of who gets to speculate about whose future
5. **PD + Design Justice → Equitable Design** (Costanza-Chock, 2020; DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001; Harrington et al., 2019; DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300233): Participation reframed through justice and equity

### Tensions and Debates
1. **Codification vs. Diversity** in RtD: Zimmerman et al. (DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240704) formalize vs. Gaver (DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889) keep open
2. **Provocation vs. Solution** across lineages: speculative design (provoke) vs. PD (solve with people) vs. transition design (systemic change)
3. **Who participates?** PD's expanding circle: workers → users → communities → marginalized groups → nonhumans
4. **Eurocentrism** in speculative design: Prado & Oliveira (2014) and Schultz et al. (2018; DOI: 10.1016/j.destud.2018.05.001) challenge whose speculation counts
5. **What counts as knowledge?** Annotated portfolios (DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889) vs. strong concepts (DOI: 10.1145/2362364.2362371) vs. design fiction vs. critical making — competing models of research contribution

## Temporal Phases

### Phase 1: Foundations (1983–1999)
Schön, Cross (DOI: 10.1016/0142-694X(82)90040-0), Frayling, Ehn (DOI: 10.1201/9780203743461), Scandinavian PD. Design research establishes epistemological legitimacy.

### Phase 2: Formalization (2000–2009)
RtD formalized for HCI (Zimmerman 2007; DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240704). Critical design crystallizes (Dunne 1999). Design fiction coined (Sterling 2005, Bleecker 2009). Co-design codified (Sanders & Stappers 2008; DOI: 10.1080/15710880701875068).

### Phase 3: Diversification (2010–2016)
Explosion of new lineages: critical making (Ratto 2011; DOI: 10.1080/01972243.2011.583819), adversarial design (DiSalvo 2012), annotated portfolios (Gaver & Bowers 2012; DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889), strong concepts (Höök & Löwgren 2012; DOI: 10.1145/2362364.2362371), transition design (Irwin 2015; DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829). Intermediate knowledge forms proliferate.

### Phase 4: Posthuman Turn (2017–present)
More-than-human design (Forlano 2017; DOI: 10.1016/j.sheji.2017.08.001; Wakkary 2021; ISBN: 978-0262542999), entanglement HCI (Frauenberger 2019; DOI: 10.1145/3364998), decolonial design (Escobar 2018; DOI: 10.1215/9780822371090). Design research decenters the human, questions Western assumptions, and extends agency to things, species, and systems.

## Implications for the Paper

1. **The genealogy is a network, not a tree.** While we use tree metaphors, the reality is cross-pollination, convergence, and feedback loops. Our visualization should reflect this.

2. **PD is the deepest root.** Participatory design predates Frayling and provides the democratic ethos that permeates many other lineages. It is not a branch — it is a parallel trunk.

3. **The posthuman turn is the current frontier.** More-than-human design, entanglement HCI, and decolonial design represent the most active areas of methodological innovation.

4. **Tensions are productive.** The debates (formalization vs. openness, human vs. nonhuman, Western vs. pluriversal) are not problems to solve but generative forces that drive methodological evolution.

5. **Missing lineages to investigate:** Somaesthetic design (Höök), autobiographical design (Neustaedter & Sengers), design activism (Fuad-Luke), ludic design (Gaver). These may deserve their own branches or may be sub-branches of existing lineages.
