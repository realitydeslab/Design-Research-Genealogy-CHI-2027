# Design Research Genealogy: Draft Mapping

## Overview

This document maps the genealogical relationships between design research methodologies — how they emerged from, influenced, diverged from, and converged with each other. The genealogy spans from 1983 (Schön's *Reflective Practitioner*) through 2026, covering the major lineages identified in our literature review.

---

## Root Ancestors (Pre-1993)

These works predate the formal taxonomy of design research methodologies but provide the intellectual soil from which all lineages grow:

- **Schön (1983)** — *The Reflective Practitioner*: Established reflection-in-action as a mode of professional knowing. Foundation for all practice-based design research.
- **Scandinavian PD movement (1970s–80s)** — Workplace democracy + cooperative systems design (Ehn 1988, Bødker 1991, Greenbaum & Kyng 1991). Foundation for participatory and democratic approaches.
- **Cross (1982, 2001, 2006)** — *Designerly Ways of Knowing*: Argued design is a distinct intellectual culture with its own epistemology. Grounded the claim that design research is legitimate.
- **Archer (1995)** — Parallel UK articulation of research in/through/for design.

---

## The Trunk: Frayling's Taxonomy (1993)

**Frayling, C. (1993). Research in Art and Design.**

The originating taxonomy: research *into*, *through*, and *for* art and design. This single paper is the common ancestor for virtually all design research methodologies that followed. Every lineage either builds on or reacts against it.

**Branching point:** Frayling's "research through design" became the name of an entire movement, but his other categories (*into* and *for*) also spawned distinct research programs.

## Major Lineages and Branching Points

### Lineage 1: Participatory Design (1970s → present)

**Timeline:** 1970s Scandinavian origins → 1988 Ehn → 1991 Greenbaum & Kyng → 1993 Schuler & Namioka → 1999 cultural probes (Gaver, Dunne, Pacenti) → 2008 co-design (Sanders & Stappers) → 2010 design things (Ehn) → 2013 Simonsen & Robertson handbook → 2019 design justice (Costanza-Chock) → 2020 more-than-human participation (Akama et al.)

**Key branching points:**
- **1999:** Cultural probes (Gaver, Dunne & Pacenti) branch participatory methods into more exploratory, aesthetic territory → eventually feeds into RtD and speculative design
- **2008:** Sanders & Stappers codify "co-design" as distinct from traditional PD → broader adoption outside Scandinavian tradition
- **2010:** Ehn's "design things" bridges PD with ANT/STS → seeds more-than-human participatory design
- **2019–20:** Costanza-Chock's design justice + Akama et al.'s more-than-human participation → PD branches toward social justice and posthumanism

**Descendants:** Co-design, design justice, infrastructuring, more-than-human participation

---

### Lineage 2: Research through Design (1993 → present)

**Timeline:** 1993 Frayling → 2003 Fallman → 2007 Zimmerman et al. (CHI formalization) → 2008 Zimmerman & Forlizzi → 2010 Zimmerman, Stolterman, Forlizzi → 2011 Koskinen et al. → 2012 Gaver (annotated portfolios) → 2012 Höök & Löwgren (strong concepts) → 2017 Redström → 2019 Frauenberger (entanglement)

**Key branching points:**
- **2007:** Zimmerman et al. formalize RtD for HCI → creates the "HCI-flavored RtD" branch distinct from art/design school RtD
- **2011:** Koskinen et al.'s *lab/field/showroom* taxonomy → constructive design research as a broader umbrella
- **2012:** Gaver & Bowers' annotated portfolios + Höök & Löwgren's strong concepts → two competing models for intermediate knowledge, both still active
- **2017:** Redström's *Making Design Theory* → programmatic approach to design theory construction
- **2019:** Frauenberger's entanglement HCI → RtD meets posthumanism/new materialism, bridging to more-than-human design

**Descendants:** Annotated portfolios, strong concepts, concept-driven design research, constructive design research, entanglement HCI

---

### Lineage 3: Critical & Speculative Design (1999 → present)

**Timeline:** 1999 Dunne (*Hertzian Tales*) → 2001 Dunne & Raby (*Design Noir*) → 2005 Sterling (*Shaping Things*) → 2009 Bleecker (design fiction) → 2011 Ratto (critical making) → 2012 DiSalvo (*Adversarial Design*) → 2013 Dunne & Raby (*Speculative Everything*) → 2013 Bardzell & Bardzell (critique of critical design) → 2017 Malpass → 2018 decolonizing critiques (Schultz et al.)

**Key branching points:**
- **1999–2001:** Dunne's *Hertzian Tales* + *Design Noir* establish critical design as a distinct practice → separates from RtD by emphasizing provocation over problem-solving
- **2005–2009:** Sterling and Bleecker coin and formalize **design fiction** → branches from critical design by emphasizing narrative/diegetic prototypes over gallery objects
- **2011:** Ratto's **critical making** → bridges critical design with material practice; making as critical thinking
- **2012:** DiSalvo's **adversarial design** → politicizes critical design through agonistic theory (Mouffe)
- **2013:** *Speculative Everything* popularizes speculative design → simultaneously canonizes and crystallizes it, inviting critique
- **2014–18:** Prado & Oliveira, Schultz et al. → **decolonizing critiques** challenge SCD's Eurocentrism → leads to decolonial design futures
- **2017:** Malpass distinguishes associative, speculative, and critical design → first systematic internal taxonomy

**Descendants:** Design fiction, critical making, adversarial design, speculative enactments, decolonial design

---

### Lineage 4: Design Fiction (2005 → present)

**Parent lineage:** Branches from Speculative & Critical Design

**Timeline:** 2005 Sterling → 2009 Bleecker → 2010 Kirby (diegetic prototypes) → 2014 Blythe (fictional abstracts), Tanenbaum (narrative HCI), Lindley & Sharma (anticipatory ethnography) → 2015 Lindley & Coulton (decade retrospective) → 2016 Lindley & Coulton (fictional research papers) → 2017 Elsden et al. (speculative enactments) → 2019 Coulton & Lindley (IoT design fiction)

**Key branching points:**
- **2010:** Kirby's "diegetic prototypes" grounds design fiction in film studies → gives theoretical legitimacy
- **2014:** Multiple CHI papers → design fiction enters HCI mainstream
- **2017:** Elsden et al.'s "speculative enactments" → design fiction merges with user study methods → bridges to participatory futures

**Convergence:** Design fiction increasingly converges with experiential futures (Candy) and participatory methods

---

### Lineage 5: More-than-Human Design (2016 → present)

**Parent lineages:** Draws from posthumanist theory (Haraway, Barad, Braidotti), ANT (Latour), RtD, and PD

**Timeline:** 2005 Latour (ANT) + Verbeek (postphenomenology) → 2016 Giaccardi et al. (thing ethnography), Forlano (decentering) → 2017 Forlano (*Posthumanism and Design*), Wakkary et al. (Morse Things) → 2019 Giaccardi & Redström, Clarke et al., Frauenberger (entanglement) → 2020 Akama et al. (more-than-human participation) → 2021 Wakkary (*Things We Could Design*) → 2022 Oogjes & Wakkary (weaving stories)

**Key branching points:**
- **2016–17:** Forlano, Giaccardi, and Wakkary independently articulate more-than-human design → convergent emergence from different philosophical roots
- **2019:** Giaccardi & Redström name "more-than-human design" + Coulton & Lindley bring OOO → crystallization of the field
- **2019:** Frauenberger's entanglement HCI → bridge between RtD and more-than-human → Barad enters HCI
- **2020–21:** Wakkary's book + Akama et al.'s more-than-human participation → branches into both thing-centered and multispecies directions

**Sub-branches:**
- **Thing-centered design:** Giaccardi, Wakkary, Redström → IoT, computational things
- **Multispecies design:** Westerlaken, Mancini (ACI) → animals, ecosystems
- **Posthuman HCI:** Frauenberger → entanglement, agential realism in HCI

---

### Lineage 6: Futuring & Transition Design (2006 → present)

**Parent lineages:** Futures studies (Dator, Bell, Slaughter), design for sustainability, PD

**Timeline:** 2006 Willis (ontological designing) → 2009 Fry (*Design Futuring*) → 2010 Candy (experiential futures) → 2015 Irwin (transition design) → 2015 Manzini (design for social innovation) → 2017 Candy & Dunagan → 2018 Escobar (*Designs for the Pluriverse*) → 2019 Mazé (politics of design futures), Candy & Kornet (ethnographic experiential futures)

**Key branching points:**
- **2006–09:** Willis and Fry establish "ontological designing" and "design futuring" → design itself has futural agency
- **2010:** Candy's experiential futures → makes futures tangible through design; bridges design fiction with foresight
- **2015:** Irwin's transition design → ambitious synthesis of sustainability science, systems thinking, and design → CMU school
- **2018:** Escobar's *Designs for the Pluriverse* → decolonial design futures → challenges Western futuring dominance

**Convergence with other lineages:**
- Experiential futures ↔ design fiction (shared interest in materializing futures)
- Transition design ↔ PD (shared interest in democratic participation in change)
- Decolonial design ↔ design justice (shared critique of power asymmetries)

---

### Lineage 7: Feminist HCI & Design Justice (2010 → present)

**Cross-cutting lineage** — not a single genealogical branch but a critical lens that intersects multiple lineages:

**Timeline:** 2010 Bardzell (*Feminist HCI*) → 2013 Bardzell & Bardzell (critical design analysis) → 2015 Bardzell et al. (*Immodest Proposals*) → 2019 Harrington et al. (equitable PD) → 2020 Costanza-Chock (*Design Justice*)

**Intersections:**
- With RtD: Bardzell et al. (2015) on how RtD generates knowledge
- With critical design: Bardzell & Bardzell (2013) on what "critical" means
- With PD: Harrington et al. (2019) on equitable participation
- With futures: Appadurai (2013) on unequal capacity to aspire

## Key Cross-Lineage Influences

### Convergences
1. **RtD + Posthumanism → Entanglement HCI** (Frauenberger, 2019): RtD's practice-based epistemology meets Barad's agential realism
2. **Design Fiction + Experiential Futures → Speculative Enactments** (Elsden et al., 2017): Narrative speculation meets immersive scenario design
3. **PD + More-than-Human → More-than-Human Participation** (Akama et al., 2020): Democratic participation extended to nonhuman entities
4. **Critical Design + Futures Studies → Decolonial Design** (Escobar, 2018; Schultz et al., 2018): Critique of who gets to speculate about whose future
5. **PD + Design Justice → Equitable Design** (Costanza-Chock, 2020; Harrington et al., 2019): Participation reframed through justice and equity

### Tensions and Debates
1. **Codification vs. Diversity** in RtD: Zimmerman et al. (formalize) vs. Gaver (keep open)
2. **Provocation vs. Solution** across lineages: speculative design (provoke) vs. PD (solve with people) vs. transition design (systemic change)
3. **Who participates?** PD's expanding circle: workers → users → communities → marginalized groups → nonhumans
4. **Eurocentrism** in speculative design: Prado & Oliveira (2014) and Schultz et al. (2018) challenge whose speculation counts
5. **What counts as knowledge?** Annotated portfolios vs. strong concepts vs. design fiction vs. critical making — competing models of research contribution

## Temporal Phases

### Phase 1: Foundations (1983–1999)
Schön, Cross, Frayling, Ehn, Scandinavian PD. Design research establishes epistemological legitimacy.

### Phase 2: Formalization (2000–2009)
RtD formalized for HCI (Zimmerman 2007). Critical design crystallizes (Dunne 1999). Design fiction coined (Sterling 2005, Bleecker 2009). Co-design codified (Sanders & Stappers 2008).

### Phase 3: Diversification (2010–2016)
Explosion of new lineages: critical making (2011), adversarial design (2012), annotated portfolios (2012), strong concepts (2012), transition design (2015). Intermediate knowledge forms proliferate.

### Phase 4: Posthuman Turn (2017–present)
More-than-human design (Forlano 2017, Wakkary 2021), entanglement HCI (Frauenberger 2019), decolonial design (Escobar 2018). Design research decenters the human, questions Western assumptions, and extends agency to things, species, and systems.

## Implications for the Paper

1. **The genealogy is a network, not a tree.** While we use tree metaphors, the reality is cross-pollination, convergence, and feedback loops. Our visualization should reflect this.

2. **PD is the deepest root.** Participatory design predates Frayling and provides the democratic ethos that permeates many other lineages. It is not a branch — it is a parallel trunk.

3. **The posthuman turn is the current frontier.** More-than-human design, entanglement HCI, and decolonial design represent the most active areas of methodological innovation.

4. **Tensions are productive.** The debates (formalization vs. openness, human vs. nonhuman, Western vs. pluriversal) are not problems to solve but generative forces that drive methodological evolution.

5. **Missing lineages to investigate:** Somaesthetic design (Höök), autobiographical design (Neustaedter & Sengers), design activism (Fuad-Luke), ludic design (Gaver). These may deserve their own branches or may be sub-branches of existing lineages.
