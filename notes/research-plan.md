# Research Plan: PRISMA Scoping Review Protocol

## Title
Design Research Genealogy: A Systematic Scoping Review of Methodologies and Works

## Research Questions

**RQ1:** What are the major design research methodologies that have emerged in HCI and design studies since 1993?

**RQ2:** What are the genealogical relationships (influences, divergences, branching points) between these methodologies?

**RQ3:** How have design research methodologies evolved over time, and what are the key inflection points?

---

## Method: PRISMA-ScR Scoping Review

We follow the PRISMA-ScR guidelines (Tricco et al., 2018; DOI: 10.7326/M18-0850) and the Arksey & O'Malley (2005; DOI: 10.1080/1364557032000119616) / Levac et al. (2010; DOI: 10.1186/1748-5908-5-69) framework for scoping reviews.

### Stage 1: Identifying the Research Question
- See RQ1–RQ3 above.
- Population: Design research methodologies in HCI and design studies
- Concept: Genealogical relationships between methods
- Context: Academic publications 1993–2026

### Stage 2: Identifying Relevant Studies

**Databases:**
- ACM Digital Library
- IEEE Xplore
- Scopus
- Web of Science
- Google Scholar (supplementary)
- Design Research Society (DRS) archive

**Search Terms:**
Primary: ("research through design" OR "research-through-design" OR "constructive design research" OR "speculative design" OR "critical design" OR "design fiction" OR "participatory design" OR "co-design" OR "more-than-human design" OR "posthuman design" OR "transition design" OR "design futures" OR "adversarial design" OR "critical making" OR "design probes" OR "annotated portfolio")

Combined with: ("methodology" OR "method" OR "approach" OR "framework" OR "practice" OR "epistemology")

Context filter: ("HCI" OR "human-computer interaction" OR "interaction design" OR "design research" OR "design studies")

**Time range:** 1993–2026 (from Frayling's foundational paper to present)

**Language:** English

### Stage 3: Study Selection

**Inclusion Criteria:**
- IC1: Proposes, defines, or substantially reflects on a design research methodology
- IC2: Published in peer-reviewed venue (conference, journal, or book chapter)
- IC3: Addresses methodology at a conceptual/theoretical level (not purely application)
- IC4: Published 1993–2026
- IC5: Written in English

**Exclusion Criteria:**
- EC1: Pure application papers that use a method without methodological reflection
- EC2: Workshop proposals, extended abstracts, or posters (unless seminal)
- EC3: Non-design fields (e.g., pure engineering, pure social science) unless explicitly addressing design research
- EC4: Duplicate publications of the same work

**Screening Process:**
1. Title/abstract screening (two reviewers, inter-rater reliability)
2. Full-text screening against IC/EC criteria
3. Snowball sampling from reference lists of included papers
4. Expert consultation for completeness check

### Stage 4: Charting the Data

**Data Extraction Fields:**
- Paper ID, authors, year, venue
- Methodology named/proposed
- Parent/ancestor methodologies cited
- Key concepts introduced
- Disciplinary origin (HCI, design studies, art, STS, etc.)
- Epistemological orientation (constructivist, pragmatist, critical, posthumanist, etc.)
- Type of contribution (foundational, extension, critique, synthesis, application)
- Genealogical relationships (influences, builds upon, diverges from, synthesizes)

### Stage 5: Collating, Summarizing, and Reporting

**Analysis:**
1. **Temporal mapping:** When did each methodology emerge? What was the publication trajectory?
2. **Genealogical network:** Which methods cite which as ancestors? Where do lineages branch?
3. **Thematic analysis:** What recurring concerns (rigor, participation, agency, futures, critique) cut across lineages?
4. **Inflection point analysis:** What events/papers caused branching or convergence?

**Visualization:**
- Genealogical tree diagram showing lineage relationships
- Timeline of key publications and branching points
- Network graph of citation relationships between methodological papers
- Sankey/alluvial diagram showing methodological evolution

## Identified Methodological Lineages (Preliminary)

Based on our literature review, we identify the following major lineages:

1. **Research through Design (RtD)** — Frayling (1993) → Zimmerman et al. (2007; DOI: 10.1145/1240624.1240704) → Gaver & Bowers (2012; DOI: 10.1145/2212877.2212889) → annotated portfolios, strong concepts (Höök & Löwgren 2012; DOI: 10.1145/2362364.2362371)
2. **Speculative & Critical Design** — Dunne (1999; ISBN: 978-0262541992) → Dunne & Raby (2013; ISBN: 978-0262019842) → branches into design fiction, adversarial design (DiSalvo 2012; ISBN: 978-0262017381), critical making (Ratto 2011; DOI: 10.1080/01972243.2011.583819)
3. **Design Fiction** — Sterling (2005; ISBN: 978-0262693264) → Bleecker (2009) → Lindley & Coulton (2015; DOI: 10.1145/2783446.2783592)
4. **Participatory Design** — Ehn (1988; DOI: 10.1201/9780203743461) → Sanders & Stappers (2008; DOI: 10.1080/15710880701875068) → design justice (Costanza-Chock 2020; DOI: 10.7551/mitpress/12255.001.0001), infrastructuring
5. **More-than-Human Design** — Haraway/Barad (theory) → Forlano (2017; DOI: 10.1016/j.sheji.2017.08.001) → Wakkary (2021; ISBN: 978-0262542999) → Giaccardi & Redström (2020; DOI: 10.1162/desi_a_00612)
6. **Transition Design** — Irwin (2015; DOI: 10.1080/17547075.2015.1051829) → sustainability transitions, systemic design
7. **Design Futures / Experiential Futures** — Dator → Candy (2010; DOI: 10.13140/RG.2.1.1840.0248) → experiential scenarios
8. **Feminist HCI** — Bardzell (2010; DOI: 10.1145/1753326.1753521) → intersects with critical design, PD, more-than-human
9. **Constructive Design Research** — Koskinen et al. (2011; DOI: 10.1016/C2010-0-65896-2) — lab/field/showroom taxonomy
10. **Ontological Design** — Willis (2006; DOI: 10.2752/144871306X13966268131514) → Fry (2009; ISBN: 978-1847882172) → design futuring/defuturing

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Scope too broad | Focus on methodological papers, not application papers |
| Missing important lineages | Expert consultation + snowball sampling |
| Subjective genealogical mapping | Two independent coders + explicit criteria |
| Blurred boundaries between methods | Acknowledge overlap; map as network, not strict tree |
| CHI page limit | Focus genealogy diagram + key findings; full data in supplementary |
