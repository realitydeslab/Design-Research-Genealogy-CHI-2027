# Design Research Genealogy: Draft Mapping v2 (Observatory-Informed)

## Overview

This document maps the genealogical relationships between design research methodologies, now informed by the Design Research Observatory's 21-concept taxonomy. The genealogy spans from 1970s Scandinavian PD through to the posthuman turn of the 2020s, covering all 21 Observatory concepts plus additional lineages from our literature review.

**Key change from v1:** The Observatory provides an explicit structural skeleton that our v1 draft lacked. Where v1 identified 7 lineages, v2 maps 18+ interconnected lineages organized into epistemological, methodological, speculative, participatory, observational, temporal, and contextual layers.

---

## Structural Layers (Observatory-Informed)

### Layer 1: Epistemological Foundations

**Constructionism** (Papert, 1980; 1991)
- Making = learning = knowledge production
- Provides the epistemological justification for RtD's central claim
- Without constructionism, RtD has no theory of why designing produces knowledge
- Cross (1982, 2001) "designerly ways of knowing" articulates this for design specifically
- Schön (1983) "reflection-in-action" provides the practice-theoretical complement

### Layer 2: Methodological Core

**Research through Design (RtD)** (Frayling 1993 → Zimmerman et al. 2007)
- The central methodology from which speculative, critical, material, and temporal approaches branch
- The Observatory places RtD at the hub — more centrally than our v1 draft did
- Koskinen et al. (2011): lab/field/showroom taxonomy for constructive design research
- Gaver & Bowers (2012) annotated portfolios vs. Höök & Löwgren (2012) strong concepts = competing intermediate knowledge forms

**Prototyping as Research** (Houde & Hill 1997 → Buchenau & Suri 2000 → Lim et al. 2008)
- The material practice through which RtD produces knowledge
- Prototypes as epistemological instruments, not pre-products
- Connects to constructionism: making the prototype IS the inquiry

### Layer 3: Speculative Branch

**RtD → Speculative Design** (Dunne 1999 → Dunne & Raby 2013)
- Design for provocation rather than problem-solving
- The PPPP futures cone (adapted from Voros 2003 foresight framework)

**Speculative Design → Design Fiction** (Sterling 2005 → Bleecker 2009 → Lindley & Coulton 2015)
- Narrative-based speculation; diegetic prototypes (Kirby 2010)
- Design Fiction as World Building (Coulton & Lindley 2017)

**Design Fiction → World Building** (Coulton et al. 2017)
- Internally-consistent fictional worlds as design research contexts
- Distinct from storytelling: worlds, not narratives, are the unit of analysis

**Design Fiction + Ethnography → Anticipatory Ethnography** (Lindley, Sharma & Potts 2014)
- Hybrid method: ethnographic analysis of design fiction artifacts
- Extends ethnographic temporal scope into the future

**RtD → Critical Design** (Dunne 1999 → Bardzell & Bardzell 2013 → Malpass 2017)
- Design as questioning societal norms
- Malpass (2017) distinguishes associative, speculative, and critical strands

**Speculative Design → Afrofuturism** (Dery 1993 → Eshun 2003 → Harrington et al. 2022)
- Decolonial speculative practice
- Challenges SCD's Eurocentrism (Prado & Oliveira 2014; Schultz et al. 2018)

**RtD + Speculative Design → Material Speculation** (Wakkary et al. 2015)
- Functional prototypes deployed in everyday settings as critical inquiry
- Bridges the gap between gallery-based speculation and situated research

### Layer 4: Participatory Branch

**Participatory Design (PD)** (Scandinavian 1970s → Ehn 1988 → Schuler & Namioka 1993)
- Parallel trunk, not a branch of RtD — predates Frayling
- Democratic participation as design principle
- Cultural probes (Gaver, Dunne & Pacenti 1999) bridge PD and RtD

**PD ↔ Co-Design** (Sanders & Stappers 2008)
- Bidirectional relationship; sometimes interchangeable
- Co-design expands PD beyond Scandinavian workplace tradition

### Layer 5: Observational Branch

**Ethnography** (Suchman 1987 → Button & Dourish 1996 → Crabtree et al. 2012)
- Root method entering HCI through workplace studies
- Dourish (2006) "implications for design" critique

**Ethnography + More-than-Human Design → Thing Ethnography** (Giaccardi et al. 2016)
- Imagining object perspectives; things as co-ethnographers
- Practical entry point for more-than-human design

**Design Fiction + Ethnography → Anticipatory Ethnography** (Lindley et al. 2014)
- (Cross-listed with Speculative Branch)
- Ethnographic analysis of fictional futures

### Layer 6: Temporal/Reflective Branch

**Slow Technology** (Hallnäs & Redström 2001)
- Reflection and calmness over speed and efficiency
- Extended by Odom, Wakkary et al. through long-term deployed artifacts

**Foresight** (Slaughter 1995 → Voros 2003 → Candy 2010)
- Professional futures discipline providing systematic methods
- Horizon scanning, scenario planning, backcasting, CLA
- Candy's experiential futures bridges foresight and design

### Layer 7: Contextual Layer (Paradigms Challenged/Extended)

**Human-Centered Design (HCD)** (Norman 1988 → ISO 9241)
- The dominant paradigm; the origin point the posthuman turn responds to
- Norman's own self-critique (2005) as early signal

**HCD → [challenged by] → More-than-Human Design** (Forlano 2017 → Giaccardi & Redström 2020 → Wakkary 2021)
- The most prominent paradigm shift in the genealogy
- Draws on Haraway, Barad, Braidotti, Latour

**Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)**
- The disciplinary home for much design research
- Three paradigms (Harrison et al. 2007) → "fourth wave" entanglement HCI (Frauenberger 2019)

**User Research** (within HCD)
- The empirical practice that more-than-human methods extend

**IoT (Internet of Things)**
- Application domain driving Design Fiction, Thing Ethnography, Material Speculation
- Connected devices make thing-centered design practically relevant

11. **Prototyping as Research** — Material practice of knowledge production

### Structural Changes
1. **Layered architecture**: v2 organizes by epistemological, methodological, speculative, participatory, observational, temporal, and contextual layers (following Observatory's implicit structure)
2. **Constructionism as ground**: v1 had no epistemological layer; v2 begins with constructionism
3. **RtD as central hub**: Observatory's framing places RtD more centrally than v1
4. **Ethnography as root**: Treated as a foundational method branching into Thing Ethnography and Anticipatory Ethnography
5. **Explicit HCD → MtHD paradigm shift**: v1 implied this; v2 makes it the primary structural tension

### Differences from Observatory's Framing
1. **We add feminist HCI and design justice as cross-cutting lenses** — Observatory doesn't include these
2. **We add transition design and futuring** — Observatory has foresight but not Irwin's transition design
3. **We treat PD as a parallel trunk, not a branch** — Observatory links it to HCD; we give it deeper independence
4. **We emphasize temporal phases** — Observatory is synchronic; our genealogy is explicitly diachronic
5. **We add the "quality of speculation" thread** (Ringfort-Felner et al. 2023–2026) — Observatory predates this
6. **We include decolonial critiques more centrally** — Observatory mentions Afrofuturism but doesn't foreground Prado/Oliveira, Schultz, Escobar

## Implications for the Paper

1. **The genealogy is a layered network, not a tree.** The Observatory provides a synchronic map; our PRISMA review adds the diachronic dimension — when and how connections formed.

2. **Constructionism is the missing epistemological story.** Design research often argues that "making produces knowledge" without grounding this claim. Constructionism provides the theory.

3. **The HCD → More-than-Human shift is the field's primary paradigm transition.** Our genealogy documents how and why this happened — through ANT, posthumanism, and practical methods like thing ethnography.

4. **Ethnography is underrecognized as a root method.** Our genealogy shows it feeds PD, thing-centered design, anticipatory ethnography, and more — it's not just "fieldwork" but a foundational epistemological tradition.

5. **Material speculation resolves a central tension.** The gap between provocative-but-fictional (speculative design) and functional-but-conventional (RtD) is bridged by material speculation. Our genealogy shows this convergence.

6. **The field is actively trying to codify itself.** The quality of speculation thread (Ringfort-Felner et al.) and recent taxonomies (Snow et al. 2025, Cardenas et al. 2025) show the field doing the kind of self-organization our genealogy supports.
