# Target Venues

## Primary Target

1. **CHI — ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems**
   - Deadline: ~September 2026 (for CHI 2027)
   - Track: Papers (full-length, archival)
   - Fit: CHI is the premier venue for HCI research. Design research methodologies are a core topic. Systematic reviews have appeared at CHI (Lazar et al. 2017, Sanches et al. 2019). A genealogical mapping of design research methods would be of broad interest.
   - **This is our target venue.**

## Secondary / Backup Venues

2. **DIS — ACM Conference on Designing Interactive Systems**
   - Deadline: ~February (for DIS in the same year)
   - Fit: DIS is the premier venue for design research in HCI specifically. RtD, speculative design, and more-than-human design papers frequently appear here. A methodological genealogy would be highly relevant.

3. **DRS — Design Research Society Conference**
   - Deadline: ~January (biennial, next: 2026)
   - Fit: DRS spans all design research traditions, not just HCI. Broader scope makes the genealogy relevant. More design studies, less CS audience.

4. **Design Studies (Journal)**
   - Rolling submissions
   - Fit: Premier design research journal. Systematic reviews and methodological reflections are welcome. Journal format allows more depth than conference paper.

5. **Design Issues (Journal, MIT Press)**
   - Rolling submissions
   - Fit: Theory-oriented design journal. Genealogical mapping fits its intellectual orientation.

6. **CoDesign (Journal, Taylor & Francis)**
   - Rolling submissions
   - Fit: Particularly relevant for the participatory design lineage; journal welcomes methodological reflections.

7. **International Journal of Design**
   - Rolling submissions
   - Fit: Open-access journal covering design research methods; suitable for systematic review.

8. **She Ji: The Journal of Design, Economics, and Innovation**
   - Rolling submissions
   - Fit: Publishes methodological and theoretical design papers (e.g., Forlano 2017).

9. **PDC — Participatory Design Conference**
   - Deadline: ~March (biennial)
   - Fit: Relevant for the PD lineage analysis; niche audience.

10. **C&C — ACM Conference on Creativity and Cognition**
    - Deadline: ~March
    - Fit: Design fiction and speculative design papers appear here.

## Venue Strategy

**Plan A:** Submit to **CHI 2027** (deadline September 2026). CHI's broad HCI audience means the genealogy will reach researchers across all the lineages we map.

**Plan B:** If CHI reviews suggest narrower scope, adapt for **DIS** or **Design Studies** journal.

**Plan C:** For a more design-theory audience, **Design Issues** or **DRS**.
